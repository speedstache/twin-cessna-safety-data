# Narrative Summary

The aircraft departed a high-elevation airport during summer daytime conditions. Witnesses reported a long takeoff roll followed by a shallow climb. Shortly after liftoff, the aircraft began a left yaw and rolled before impacting terrain off the departure end of the runway.

Performance calculations conducted during the investigation indicated that the aircraft was near maximum gross weight and operating in high density altitude conditions. Single-engine climb performance under those conditions would have been limited.

No preimpact mechanical anomalies were identified. The pilot and passenger survived with serious injuries. The aircraft was substantially damaged.

The investigation cited inadequate airspeed control and reduced climb capability following a loss of engine power during takeoff.
