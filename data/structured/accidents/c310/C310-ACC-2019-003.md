# Narrative Summary

The aircraft was conducting an instrument approach in IMC conditions during night operations. Air traffic control radar data indicated deviations in airspeed and descent rate during the final segment of the approach.

The aircraft descended below the glidepath and impacted terrain short of the runway threshold. Weather conditions at the time included low ceilings and reduced visibility.

The investigation found no evidence of preimpact mechanical failure. The pilot held appropriate ratings and was instrument current. Recorded radar data suggested increasing workload during the approach phase.

The aircraft was destroyed upon impact, and the pilot sustained fatal injuries.
