# Narrative Summary

The accident occurred while the airplane was exiting the runway after landing at Stockton. The pilot reported that this was the first flight after an annual inspection in which the landing gear had been rigged. The landing and rollout were normal until the pilot slowed to exit the high-speed taxiway and the right main landing gear collapsed. Independent inspection found that the right main gear rigging caused gear tension to terminate prematurely and prevented the downlock link from reaching its full over-center locked position. Side loads were then carried by linkage components not designed for those loads, and the components failed in overload. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the right wing, right propeller, and right horizontal stabilizer.
