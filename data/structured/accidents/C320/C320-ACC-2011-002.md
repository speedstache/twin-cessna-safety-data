# Narrative Summary

During preflight, the pilot noticed that the left main landing gear strut appeared low and contacted a maintenance facility to service the strut and fill the oxygen bottle. After the work was completed, the pilot and passengers departed. During initial climb, the pilot heard a noise while raising the landing gear and observed that the nose gear was partially retracted. Normal and manual gear cycling did not change the nose gear position. The pilot landed with the nose gear partially retracted, and the airplane skidded to a stop on the runway. FAA inspection found the landing gear actuator push-pull tube end bolt secured to the rod end but disconnected from the nose gear door hinge clevis. The mechanic reported that the nose gear doors had been disconnected to access the oxygen bottle valve and control cable.
