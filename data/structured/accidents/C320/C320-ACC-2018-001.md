# Narrative Summary

The pilot was conducting an aerial photography flight west of Lake Mountain, Utah. The planned work included three photo runs at about 7,255 feet msl before returning to Provo for fuel. After the second photo run, onboard data showed the airplane continuing southeast toward mountainous terrain and then entering a left turn about 800 feet above the terrain. During the turn, bank angle increased while the airplane climbed slightly and accelerated. The last recorded data showed about 177 knots groundspeed and about 71 degrees of left bank. The airplane impacted mountainous terrain in a near-vertical nose-down attitude. Examination found no airframe or engine anomalies that would have precluded normal operation, and engine signatures indicated power at impact.
