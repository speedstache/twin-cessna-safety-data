# Narrative Summary

The airplane approached Fallon Municipal Airport from the southwest and crossed near midfield to enter the left traffic pattern. Witnesses observed a right 270-degree turn to enter downwind, followed by a sharp left turn and descent near a close-in base turn position. Radar-derived groundspeeds on downwind were in a range where indicated airspeed could have been near potential stall speeds. The airplane impacted terrain in a nose-low and left-wing-low attitude. No engine anomalies were found that would have prevented normal operation. Weather observations near the airport included strong and gusting winds. The investigation found no records or witnesses indicating recent training in the accident make and model or in multiengine airplanes with differential thrust.
