# Narrative Summary

The non-instrument-rated pilot departed on a VFR cross-country flight over mountainous terrain. Mixed rain and snow showers were reported in the area, and radar returns showed the airplane cruising between 10,000 and 12,000 feet msl before entering a descending 720-degree turn. The radar track then showed multiple heading and altitude changes over about two minutes. The wreckage was distributed over a debris field about 0.4 mile long. Examination of wing and horizontal stabilizer spars showed overstress separation in flight with no evidence of fatigue. The NTSB found that the pilot failed to maintain control while maneuvering in marginal weather, which exceeded the airplane design stress limits. The pilot did not hold an instrument rating and had reported 20 hours in the preceding six months on his last medical application.
