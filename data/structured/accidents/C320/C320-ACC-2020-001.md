# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed Lago Vista, Texas, and later the airplane was observed flying low and erratically near Round Rock. Witnesses reported flames from the left engine and sputtering or popping sounds. The airplane descended through trees, impacted terrain, and was mostly consumed by postimpact fire. Examination found that the right magneto installed on the left engine would not rotate because of a failed bearing with signatures consistent with loss of lubrication. The NTSB found that the airplane likely experienced at least a partial loss of left engine power and that the pilot attempted to shut down that engine. Both exhaust systems were in poor and unairworthy condition with thin metal, holes, cracks, and oxidative degradation. The pilot had texted that the airplane had been down for leaking fuel selectors and that he had fixed wiring, and maintenance records showed long gaps between annual inspections before the most recent annual.
