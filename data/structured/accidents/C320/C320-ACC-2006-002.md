# Narrative Summary

The airplane departed from a 3,000-foot turf runway at Wimauma Air Park after landing and fueling at the self-service station. The pilot stated that a passenger fueled the airplane and that he did not know which tanks were fueled or the amounts. During the initial climb, the airplane lifted off, stopped climbing, and began sinking. The pilot selected the landing gear up to reduce drag and did not recall a loss of engine power. The airplane veered from the runway centerline and struck a telephone pole and wires about 25 feet above the ground. Examination of both engines did not reveal abnormalities that would have prevented rated power. The estimated combined weight of occupants, fuel, and items exceeded the useful load shown for the airplane even with the vortex-generator useful-load increase.
