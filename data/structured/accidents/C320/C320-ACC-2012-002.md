# Narrative Summary

The non-instrument-rated private pilot departed San Marcos Municipal Airport in night instrument meteorological conditions. An FBO employee could not see the airplane taxi because of fog. Shortly after takeoff, the airplane crashed less than one mile south of the airport and witnesses saw and heard the resulting explosion. Video evidence showed the airplane impacting in about a 45-degree nose-down attitude. Examination of the airplane and engines did not reveal mechanical anomalies that would have precluded normal operation. Weather at the airport included low ceilings and visibility that decreased to one-half mile shortly after the accident. The pilot had about 29 hours as pilot in command in the accident airplane and about 5 hours of actual instrument time.
