# Narrative Summary

The airplane departed on a maintenance test flight after the owner had spent about four years restoring it. The flight completed one circuit of the traffic pattern and began a second circuit. Witnesses observed the airplane on downwind with the landing gear extended and then saw it enter a steep nose-down attitude while turning toward base. Radar data showed groundspeed decreasing throughout the maneuver and approaching stall speed near the time of the accident. Examination of the airplane and both engines revealed no preaccident mechanical deficiencies. Toxicology findings showed medications in both pilots, and the investigation could not determine which pilot was flying when control was lost. The NTSB determined that the crew failed to maintain adequate airspeed, resulting in an aerodynamic stall close to the ground.
