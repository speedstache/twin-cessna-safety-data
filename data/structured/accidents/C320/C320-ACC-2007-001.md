# Narrative Summary

During an instructional training flight at McKinney, Texas, the airplane touched down on runway 17 during touch-and-go operations. Immediately after touchdown, the right side of the airplane slowly lowered. The pilots applied left aileron and left brake to limit yaw. The airplane departed the right side of the runway, entered the grass and a shallow ditch, and stopped with the right gear collapsed. Both occupants exited without injury. FAA postaccident inspection found that the right landing gear over-center downlock failed on touchdown. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the right wing and right horizontal stabilizer.
