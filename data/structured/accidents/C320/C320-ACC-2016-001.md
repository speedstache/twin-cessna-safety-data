# Narrative Summary

The commercial pilot and crew were conducting aerial photography under contract when the pilot reported a right-side engine vibration after departure from Buena Vista. The pilot returned to the airport and inspected the airplane with a mechanic. The crew had agreed to discontinue aerial photography work and return to the operator base, but no further update was received before departure. GPS data showed the airplane departing and then proceeding south, away from the operator base, and overflying a survey point near Creede. During the final minutes, the airplane circled near the airport while descending and slowing. Witnesses described the airplane as low or poorly positioned for the pattern, and the airplane impacted terrain. Postaccident engine test-runs and examinations found no mechanical failures that would have precluded normal operation, and the reason for the power loss was not determined.
