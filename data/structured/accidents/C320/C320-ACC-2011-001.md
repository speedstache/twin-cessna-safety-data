# Narrative Summary

The airplane was on a cross-country flight when it impacted Heron Lake near Los Ojos, New Mexico. A witness on the lake heard the airplane pass low overhead, then heard the engine sound stop and saw a splash. The airplane was not recovered from the lake, and only fragmented parts were recovered. Rain and thunderstorms were reported in the area at the time of the accident. FAA records showed that the pilot held private single-engine and multiengine ratings but did not hold an instrument rating. The NTSB found the accident consistent with controlled flight into terrain while operating at low altitude in instrument meteorological conditions. The pilot logbook was not recovered, and time in make and model could not be determined.
