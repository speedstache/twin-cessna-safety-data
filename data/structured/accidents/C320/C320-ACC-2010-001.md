# Narrative Summary

During an instructional flight, the pilots completed four touch-and-go landings and then proceeded to another airport for a soft-field landing. When the landing gear was extended, the gear-down-and-locked light did not illuminate. The annunciator lights tested normally, and mirror checks showed the nose landing gear was only partially extended. The pilots cycled the gear several times and attempted manual extension at least three times. The instructor landed at Denton Municipal Airport and the nose gear folded. Postaccident inspection revealed that the push-pull rod connecting the gear transmission to the nose gear had separated. The separation damaged the wing spar and several fuselage ribs.
