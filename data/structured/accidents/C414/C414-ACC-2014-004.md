# Narrative Summary

On August 15, 2014, a Cessna 414 was on a Part 91 personal flight to Bowie Municipal Airport in Texas.

While about 500 feet above ground level on a left base for landing, the airplane suddenly pointed down, spun, and completed about three rotations before impact.

The airplane impacted terrain in a partially nose-down attitude and was mostly consumed by postimpact fire.

Postaccident examination found no evidence of preimpact mechanical malfunctions or failures that would have precluded normal operation.

GPS data indicated that the airplane airspeed decayed below the manufacturer recommended turbulent-condition approach speed while on base leg.

A nearby pipeline patrol pilot reported severe-to-extreme turbulence and strong low-level thermals in the area.

Autopsy findings identified significant cardiovascular disease and evidence of an acute cardiac event.

The NTSB determined that pilot incapacitation from an acute cardiac event resulted in loss of control and collision with terrain.
