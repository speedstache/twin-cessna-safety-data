# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414 was operated under Part 91 to Vici Municipal Airport in Oklahoma. The pilot stated that he was landing on a short runway and could not stop the airplane during the landing rollout. The airplane overran the end of the 2,565-foot runway at about 10 to 15 miles per hour. The pilot steered to miss a four-inch pipe on the extended runway centerline. The right main landing gear struck the pipe, collapsed, and the airplane came to rest in a shallow ditch and on a road. Police skid-mark analysis indicated that the airplane landed about 300 feet down the runway. An FAA inspector computed a landing roll of approximately 2,200 feet. Flight control continuity was confirmed and no system anomalies were found. The NTSB attributed the accident to the pilot's failure to ensure adequate runway remaining during landing.
