# Narrative Summary

On September 3, 2025, the Cessna 414 was descending to land near New Braunfels, Texas. The airplane was about four miles from the destination airport when it struck a bird. The pilot continued and landed the airplane without further incident. Postflight inspection found substantial damage to the right wing. Bird remains were submitted to the Smithsonian Institution and identified as black vulture. The report noted that the average weight of a black vulture is between 3.5 and 6.6 pounds. Birdcast data indicated bird activity between medium and high at the time of the accident. The U.S. Air Force avian hazard advisory system showed severe bird-strike risk near the collision area. Both occupants were uninjured.
