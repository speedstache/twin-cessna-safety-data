# Narrative Summary

On October 8, 2020, the Cessna 414 began a Part 91 personal flight from North Palm Beach County General Aviation Airport. The pilot initiated the takeoff after run-up and taxi. During the takeoff roll, the airplane remained on the runway beyond the expected liftoff point and both pilots were unable to move the control yoke aft. The copilot reduced power and applied maximum braking, but the abort was delayed and the airplane overran the runway into rough, marshy terrain. Postaccident examination found no preimpact mechanical malfunction of the elevator system and confirmed elevator continuity and normal function. A flight control lock was found on the cockpit floor near the left rudder pedals. Video analysis showed the elevator position during taxi was similar to an exemplar airplane with the control lock installed. The manufacturer’s checklists required removal of the control lock and flight-control checks before takeoff. The airplane sustained substantial damage and all seven occupants received serious injuries.
