# Narrative Summary

On January 18, 2023, the Cessna 414 departed Modesto, California, for a Part 91 personal flight. Shortly after departure, the pilot was instructed to change from tower to departure control frequency. The pilot continued transmitting on the tower frequency and reported that the radio would not change frequencies. The pilot requested and received approval to return to the departure airport. During the return, the airplane flew a nonstandard pattern and descended as low as 200 feet pressure altitude while maneuvering near the runway. ADS-B data showed groundspeed decreased to about 53 knots during the final left turn. Witnesses observed unusual maneuvering and a stall/spin before impact. Postaccident examination of the airframe and available engine data found no preimpact mechanical malfunction that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB found that distraction from the non-critical radio anomaly contributed to the loss of control. The pilot was fatally injured and the airplane sustained substantial damage.
