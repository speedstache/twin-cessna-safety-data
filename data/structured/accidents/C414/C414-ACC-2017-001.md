# Narrative Summary

On April 7, 2017, a Cessna 414 was approaching Fulton County Airport-Brown Field during a Part 91 personal flight.

The pilot reported gusting wind conditions and a runway change from runway 26 to runway 32.

While the airplane was on a one-mile final, another airplane was cleared for takeoff on the runway.

The pilot reported being distracted by the wind, runway reconfiguration, and the airplane departing ahead.

He did not recall performing normal before-landing callouts or confirming the fuel selector, landing gear, and flaps.

A witness reported that the landing gear was retracted as the airplane approached the runway.

The airplane landed gear-up, the propellers contacted the runway, and the airplane skidded about 650 feet.

The NTSB determined that diverted attention during the landing approach resulted in failure to configure the landing gear.
