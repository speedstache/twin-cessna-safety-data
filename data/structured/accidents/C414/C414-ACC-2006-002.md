# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414A was operated on a Part 91 executive/corporate flight from Stuart, Florida, to Fort Lauderdale Executive Airport. The pilot reported departing with 45 gallons of fuel and a cruise fuel flow of about 19 gallons per hour per engine. Near the destination, the right engine began to surge, and the pilot requested priority handling but initially did not declare an emergency. After returning to downwind, the left engine also began experiencing problems. The pilot declared an emergency and landed on a road when the airplane could not reach the airport. Postaccident inspection found no visible fuel in the left wing tank and only trace or small amounts of fuel in engine fuel system components. The information manual listed total unusable fuel as 9.4 gallons. The NTSB attributed the accident to inadequate preflight planning and fuel exhaustion in both engines.
