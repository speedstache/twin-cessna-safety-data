# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414A was operated as a Part 91 personal flight from Santa Barbara to Ontario, California. After completing an ILS approach, the airplane rolled out on the runway. The pilot intended to retract the flaps but moved the landing gear lever to the retracted position. The gear warning horn activated and the pilot moved the gear handle back down. The nosewheel partially retracted before the gear could return to the down position. The airplane sustained firewall damage. The pilot reported no mechanical malfunctions or failures during the flight. The NTSB attributed the accident to the pilot's inadvertent landing gear retraction during the landing roll.
