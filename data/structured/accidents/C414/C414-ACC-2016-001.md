# Narrative Summary

On August 2, 2016, a Cessna 414 departed Destin Executive Airport on an IFR Part 91 personal flight in dark night visual conditions.

The airplane departed runway 14 and was instructed to turn southwest after takeoff.

Radar data showed the airplane climbed to about 900 feet over the Gulf of Mexico before entering a descending right turn.

The last radar return showed the airplane at about 300 feet mean sea level on a westerly heading.

Witnesses saw the airplane enter a right turn with a steep bank and then descend into the water.

Postaccident examination of recovered flight controls, engines, and propellers found no evidence of preimpact failure or malfunction.

Engine monitor data did not show a loss of power from either engine.

The NTSB determined that the pilot lost control due to spatial disorientation shortly after takeoff while maneuvering over water during dark night conditions.
