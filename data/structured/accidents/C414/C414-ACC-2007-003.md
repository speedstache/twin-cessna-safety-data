# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414A was operated as a Part 91 personal flight from Iron Mountain, Michigan, to Sturgeon Bay, Wisconsin. About eight minutes before landing, the right hydraulic light illuminated on the annunciator panel. The pilot lowered the landing gear and continued to the destination. The pilot flew the final approach with half flaps because of a crosswind and selected full flaps after touchdown. The pilot reported that the brakes had little or no effect. The airplane overran the runway, crossed the grass, went through a chain-link perimeter fence, and stopped on a perimeter road. A mechanic found that both brake assemblies were in need of service, the right brake was leaking fluid, and the left brake disc and pads required replacement. The NTSB attributed the accident to worn brakes that resulted in a landing overrun.
