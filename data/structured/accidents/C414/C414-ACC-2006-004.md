# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414A was operated on a Part 91 personal cross-country flight from San Luis Obispo to North Las Vegas. Near the destination, the pilot configured the airplane for landing with the landing gear extended, flaps extended, and spoilers deployed. At 300 to 500 feet agl on final approach, the right engine lost power. The pilot closed the spoilers and reduced flap extension, but the airplane could not sustain level flight. The pilot attempted a precautionary landing on a residential street and the left wing struck a fence. During wreckage recovery, about one gallon of fuel was found in the right fuel tank. An FAA inspector found both fuel selector valves positioned to the right fuel tank and no mechanical malfunction that would have prevented normal operation. The NTSB attributed the right engine power loss to fuel starvation from improper fuel selector positioning.
