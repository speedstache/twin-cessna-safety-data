# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414 was operated as a Part 91 executive/corporate flight from Houston, Texas, to Edwards County Airport. The airport weather near the accident time was reported as 300 feet overcast and three-quarters mile visibility in mist. Published circling minimums for the approaches available at the airport required higher weather conditions than those reported. Radar data showed the airplane completed an instrument approach to runway 14 and then initiated a left circling turn with a slight descent. The last radar target showed the airplane about 232 feet above field elevation at a groundspeed of 186 knots. Witnesses reported that the airplane circled the airport and then descended to the ground. Examination of the airframe, engines, and propellers did not identify preimpact mechanical anomalies. The NTSB attributed the accident to failure to maintain terrain clearance, with below-minimums weather and mist as contributing factors.
