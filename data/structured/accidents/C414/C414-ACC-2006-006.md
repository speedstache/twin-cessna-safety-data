# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414 was operating on a Part 91 medical positioning flight from Morgantown, West Virginia, to Teterboro, New Jersey. While en route, the pilot reported moderate mixed icing and later requested an ILS approach at Johnstown to descend and shed ice. On the approach, the pilot told the tower that whether she landed depended on whether the ice came off the airplane. The airplane broke out of the clouds and appeared to tower personnel to be initiating a missed approach before it abruptly descended toward the runway. Tower personnel observed that the landing gear was not down and instructed the pilot to go around. The airplane contacted the runway with the landing gear only partially extended and sustained damage. The pilot attempted to abort the landing, the airplane became airborne, climbed to the right, stalled, and impacted terrain. The NTSB attributed the accident to the pilot's improper decision to abort the landing with a damaged airplane, with contributing factors including the prior runway impact, failure to lower the landing gear, and icing conditions.
