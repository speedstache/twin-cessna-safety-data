# Narrative Summary

On February 3, 2019, the Cessna 414A departed Fullerton, California, on a Part 91 personal cross-country flight. Visual conditions existed at the departure airport, but weather east of the airport included precipitation, microburst activity, rain showers, and instrument meteorological conditions. The tower controller cautioned the pilot about deteriorating weather about four miles east of the airport during the takeoff clearance. Radar and recorded attitude data showed the airplane climbed before entering a rapid descending right turn. The airplane attitude varied erratically during the final seconds of flight, consistent with loss of control after entering clouds. Witnesses saw the airplane exit the clouds in a steep descent before portions of the aircraft separated in flight. Examination showed the wings and empennage separated from overstress. No preaccident mechanical anomalies were identified that explained the loss of control. The accident resulted in five fatal injuries and two serious ground injuries.
