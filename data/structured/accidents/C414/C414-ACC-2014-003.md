# Narrative Summary

On June 26, 2014, a Cessna 414 departed Creve Coeur Airport on a Part 91 personal flight.

About 15 to 20 seconds after rotation, the left front baggage door opened when the airplane was about 400 to 500 feet above ground level.

The pilot leveled the airplane and turned with the intention of returning to land.

Shortly after turning downwind, the left engine stopped producing power.

While the pilot was feathering the left engine and maintaining airspeed, the airplane struck power lines and terrain.

A postimpact explosion and fire consumed much of the airframe.

The right engine teardown revealed no preimpact anomalies.

The left engine produced full rated power during a test run, but accident damage prevented complete examination of the left engine fuel system.

The NTSB determined that the loss of left engine power occurred for reasons that could not be determined due to impact and fire damage.
