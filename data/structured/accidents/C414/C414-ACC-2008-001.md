# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414A departed Lloyd Stearman Field at night on a Part 91 personal repositioning flight. Witnesses observed the airplane flying in and out of clouds after takeoff and then turning west. One witness saw the airplane descend below the trees before flames and fireballs were observed. Weather near the airport included low ceilings, rain, and mist, and there was no official weather observation at the departure airport. The pilot had obtained multiple weather briefings before the proposed trip, including discussion of instrument conditions in the Wichita area. The pilot had an estimated 52 hours in the accident airplane and limited recent night and instrument experience. Examination of the airplane, flight controls, engines, and remaining systems revealed no anomalies. The NTSB attributed the accident to failure to maintain clearance from trees, with flight into known adverse weather, low clouds, and visibility as contributing factors.
