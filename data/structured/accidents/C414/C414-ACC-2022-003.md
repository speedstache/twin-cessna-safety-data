# Narrative Summary

On June 19, 2022, the Cessna 414 was landing at Rostraver Airport near Monongahela, Pennsylvania. The pilot reported a normal preflight, run-up, taxi, and en route portion of the flight. On final approach, the pilot adjusted the reference speed to Vref plus 10 knots due to wind shear and gusting conditions. The airplane touched down about 1,000 feet beyond the threshold on a 4,002-foot runway. The pilot applied the brakes after touchdown, but braking was not available. The airplane traveled into the grass apron, descended an embankment, and came to rest upright with substantial damage to the nose and right wing. Examination found hydraulic brake fluid in the cockpit and nose gear bay area. The nut securing a brake hose fitting at the pilot’s right brake master cylinder was loose. The NTSB determined that loss of braking authority from the loose fitting resulted in the runway overrun, but the reason the fitting loosened could not be determined.
