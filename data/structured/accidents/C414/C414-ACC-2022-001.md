# Narrative Summary

On May 2, 2022, the Cessna 414 was being operated on a local maintenance test flight from Connellsville, Pennsylvania. The pilot, who was also a mechanic, had removed and reinstalled an autopilot mode control unit before the flight. Taxi, run-up, and reported flight-control checks were normal. During the takeoff roll at rotation speed, the pilot was unable to move the control wheel aft. The pilot aborted the takeoff, but insufficient runway remained and the airplane overran the runway and struck trees. Postaccident examination found sagging avionics trays in the left cockpit avionics stack. The KX-155 tray had become lodged in the elevator bellcrank movement area. Scraping and polishing indicated contact between the tray and bellcrank had occurred before the accident flight. The trays lacked rear or side structural support straps recommended in FAA advisory guidance. The airplane sustained substantial damage, with one serious and one minor injury.
