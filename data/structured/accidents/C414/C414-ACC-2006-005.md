# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414A was operated as a Part 91 personal flight to Gwinnett County Airport-Briscoe Field. Before the first ILS approach, the pilot was advised that the weather included one-half mile visibility in fog and a 100-foot ceiling. The pilot acknowledged the weather information and continued the approach. During the first landing attempt, the pilot reported a missed approach but also said he saw the airport and wanted to attempt another approach. During the second approach, the tower controller advised that the airplane was left of the runway centerline. Shortly after the pilot acknowledged the centerline call, the airplane struck trees off the approach end of runway 25. A flight inspection found the ILS runway 25 system satisfactory. Examination of the airframe, flight controls, engines, and systems found no evidence of preimpact mechanical malfunction. The NTSB attributed the accident to failure to follow the instrument approach procedure and descent below decision height.
