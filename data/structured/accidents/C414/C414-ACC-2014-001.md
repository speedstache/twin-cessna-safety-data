# Narrative Summary

On January 23, 2014, a Cessna 414A was descending toward Jefferson City Memorial Airport during a Part 91 business flight.

The left engine experienced a partial loss of power during descent.

The pilot attempted to troubleshoot the issue, then secured the left engine and feathered the propeller when power could not be restored.

Shortly afterward, the right engine also experienced a partial power loss and eventually produced only idle power.

The pilot conducted a forced landing to a field because the airplane could not reach the destination airport.

Postaccident examination found ice in the fuel manifold valves, fuel strainer bowls, and fuel strainer screens.

The left fuel strainer bowl contained a piece of ice measuring 1 3/8 inches thick.

Fuel samples from the airplane and the fuel supplier tested negative for water contamination.

The NTSB determined that both engines failed in flight due to water contamination.
