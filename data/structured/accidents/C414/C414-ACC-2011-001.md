# Narrative Summary

On March 14, 2011, a Cessna 414 landed at Long Island MacArthur Airport after a short Part 91 personal flight.

During the landing roll, the pilot began a right turn and the right main landing gear collapsed.

The airplane departed the runway and struck a runway marker sign, resulting in substantial damage.

Postaccident examination found fractures at the right main landing gear trunnion assembly and bellcrank attachment bolt.

NTSB materials examination found overstress features and no evidence of preexisting cracking.

A serviceable right trunnion assembly had been installed during an annual inspection several days before the accident.

The airplane had operated about two hours since that installation.

Cessna engineering personnel reported that improper landing gear rigging can transmit landing loads to the trunnion lugs.

The NTSB determined that improper rigging of the right main landing gear resulted in overstress fractures of the bolt and trunnion aft lug.
