# Narrative Summary

On February 19, 2012, a Cessna 414A was conducting an IFR Part 91 personal flight to Yampa Valley Airport near Hayden, Colorado.

The pilot performed an instrument approach during an approaching winter storm.

GPS data showed the airplane crossed the approach course several times and remained below the glideslope path.

The airplane continued below the published decision height and drifted right of the runway extended centerline.

The data recorded a missed-approach attempt followed by a rapid groundspeed decrease and descent to terrain.

The airplane anti-ice and propeller anti-ice switches were found in the off position.

Weather information showed potential for moderate icing and snow in the operating area.

Examination of the airframe and engines found no preimpact anomalies that would have precluded normal operation.

The NTSB determined that an inadvertent stall during a missed approach caused the accident, with operation in forecast icing without all anti-ice systems contributing.
