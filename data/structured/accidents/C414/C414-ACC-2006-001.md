# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414A was operating as a Part 91 medical positioning flight from Honolulu to Kahului. During approach, the pilot reported a loss of engine power and radar data showed the airplane descending while maneuvering near the airport. The airplane entered multiple right turns and the calculated groundspeed decreased from 134 knots to 76 knots before impact. A sound spectrum study and propeller damage indicated one engine was operating at higher power than the other, and neither propeller was feathered. The landing gear was down and the flaps were fully deployed at impact. Engine teardown did not identify a mechanical malfunction that explained the power loss. The NTSB concluded that the pilot did not execute published procedures for configuring the airplane for single-engine flight and did not maintain Vmc. The report also identified inadequate operator training in the single-engine flight regime as a contributing factor.
