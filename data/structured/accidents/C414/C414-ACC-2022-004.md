# Narrative Summary

On September 26, 2022, the Cessna 414 was on a visual approach to Augusta Regional Airport. While crossing the runway numbers, the pilot received a landing gear unsafe warning and saw a flickering nose landing gear down light. The pilot performed a go-around and cycled the landing gear in the traffic pattern. The main landing gear lights were steady green, while the nose landing gear indication continued to flicker. The tower controller advised that the landing gear appeared down, and the pilot continued to land. On touchdown, the nose landing gear collapsed and the airplane skidded to a stop on the runway. Examination found no linkage, bellcrank, or actuator anomalies that would have caused partial nose gear extension. The manual landing gear extension crank was found in an intermediate position, but the investigation could not determine when or why it was in that position. The airplane sustained substantial fuselage damage, and the two occupants were not injured.
