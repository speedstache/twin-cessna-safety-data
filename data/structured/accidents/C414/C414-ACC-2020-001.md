# Narrative Summary

On August 30, 2020, the Cessna 414 was departing Ray Community Airport in Michigan for a repositioning flight related to annual inspection and ADS-B installation. The pilot reported normal preflight, taxi, run-up, and brake operation. During the takeoff roll, the airplane veered and the pilot attempted corrective rudder input. A pilot-rated witness reported that the airplane did not use the displaced threshold and later veered left, overcorrected right, and attempted to fly in ground effect near stall. The pilot aborted the takeoff after the airplane was already partly off the runway. The airplane traveled through grass, turned, and the landing gear collapsed. Examination was limited by impact damage, but no apparent preimpact defects were found. The NTSB determined that the pilot lost directional control during takeoff. The pilot received minor injuries and the airplane sustained substantial damage.
