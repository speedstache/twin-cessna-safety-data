# Narrative Summary

On September 28, 2017, a Cessna 414 landed at Norman Y. Mineta San Jose International Airport during a Part 91 personal flight.

The pilot was cleared to land on runway 30R and observed a large airplane on final for runway 30L while turning base.

The pilot continued the approach and reported that the landing flare was normal.

The airplane landed hard and the pilot taxied to parking.

During an annual inspection the following week, substantial damage was found to the left wing false spar and trunnion.

The pilot reported no preimpact mechanical malfunctions or failures that would have precluded normal operation.

The pilot reported 2 hours of flight time in the previous 90 days and 30 days.

The NTSB determined that failure to maintain a proper descent rate resulted in a hard landing.
