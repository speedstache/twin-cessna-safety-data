# Narrative Summary

On October 29, 2019, the Cessna 414A was conducting a GPS circling instrument approach to Linden, New Jersey, in instrument meteorological conditions. The pilot had delayed departure due to destination weather and later obtained a weather briefing that included marginal conditions and AIRMET information. During the approach, the airplane descended near minimum descent altitude while groundspeed decreased. The airplane then turned left off course and rapidly descended. Surveillance video showed the airplane in a steepening left bank near treetop level before impact. The airport remained several miles away when the airplane descended below the approach altitude. Postaccident examination found no preimpact mechanical malfunction that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB determined that the pilot failed to maintain airspeed during the circling approach, resulting in an aerodynamic stall and spin. The pilot was fatally injured and the airplane was destroyed.
