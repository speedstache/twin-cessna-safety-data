# Narrative Summary

On August 5, 2018, a Cessna 414 was nearing John Wayne Airport after a Part 91 business flight.

The controller asked whether the pilot could accept the shorter runway 20L, and the pilot initially declined.

After being instructed to hold for sequencing, the pilot stated less than a minute later that he could accept runway 20L.

The controller instructed the pilot to conduct a left 270-degree turn to enter the traffic pattern.

Flight data showed that the airplane entered a left turn, bank angle increased, and airspeed decreased.

The recorded airspeed decreased to about 59 knots, below the manufacturer published stall speed in any configuration.

The airplane entered a steep nose-down descent and impacted a shopping center parking lot about 1.6 miles from the airport.

Postaccident examination of the airframe and engines found no anomalies that would have precluded normal operation.

The NTSB determined that failure to maintain adequate airspeed while maneuvering in the traffic pattern resulted in an aerodynamic stall and spin at low altitude.
