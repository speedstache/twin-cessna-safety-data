# Narrative Summary

On April 5, 2013, a Cessna 414 was landing at Hammonton Municipal Airport after a Part 91 repositioning flight.

The pilot lowered the landing gear during the approach and observed landing gear position indicator lights indicating extension.

Immediately after touchdown, the airplane veered left and departed the runway.

The airplane struck trees and was subsequently involved in a postcrash fire.

Postaccident examination found that the nose landing gear had separated from its mount at the left trunnion.

Fractographic examination identified a preexisting crack at the surface of the left trunnion lug.

The fractured component was a used part taken from another airplane with about 20,000 service hours.

The installed trunnion assembly had received only a visual examination before installation.

The NTSB determined that failure of the nose landing gear left trunnion lug caused the landing loss of control.
