# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 414A was operated as a Part 91 business flight from Billings, Montana, to Dickinson, North Dakota. During approach, the pilot attempted to lower the landing gear and received a down-and-locked indication for the nose gear but not for either main gear. The pilot recycled the landing gear several times. The pilot then attempted the emergency gear extension procedure but still did not receive down-and-locked indications for the main gear. An individual at the airport reported that the main gear appeared to be down. During landing, the right main landing gear retracted and the airplane sustained substantial damage. A mechanic later placed the airplane on jacks, cycled the landing gear system, and found no anomalies. The NTSB attributed the accident to an undetermined failure of the emergency landing gear system and right main gear retraction during landing.
