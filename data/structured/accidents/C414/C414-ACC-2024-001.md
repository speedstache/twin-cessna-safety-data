# Narrative Summary

On July 4, 2024, the Cessna 414 landed at Lake County Airport in Leadville, Colorado, after a Part 91 personal flight. The pilot reported that the brake system operated normally during startup, taxi, and takeoff. A first landing approach ended in a go-around due to another airplane back-taxiing on the runway. On the second approach, the airplane touched down about 1,500 feet from the threshold. The pilot and copilot brake pedals had no pressure and no braking was available. The airplane rolled the length of the runway, departed the pavement while attempting to turn at the end, and the nose landing gear collapsed. Examination found the left brake master cylinder nearly empty and the right master cylinder with only about one-quarter inch of fluid remaining. Brake lines and calipers showed no leakage, but the brake linings had minimal remaining thickness and signs of overheating. Recent maintenance records noted landing gear work during the annual inspection about two months and 40 flight hours earlier. The airplane sustained substantial damage and both occupants were uninjured.
