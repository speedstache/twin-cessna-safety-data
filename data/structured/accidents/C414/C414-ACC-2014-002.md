# Narrative Summary

On June 16, 2014, a Cessna 414 was approaching Pleasanton Municipal Airport after a Part 91 flight.

Before landing, the pilot obtained airport weather that included gusting wind and recognized that either runway would involve a crosswind.

The pilot elected to land on runway 34.

On short final, the pilot realized the wind had shifted to a direct tailwind and initiated a go-around.

The pilot established a climb at about 500 feet per minute and 120 knots.

At about 200 feet above ground level, the airplane began to descend and airspeed decreased.

The airplane stalled, rolled left, and impacted the ground in a level attitude.

The pilot reported no mechanical deficiencies with the airplane or engines before the accident.

The NTSB determined that failure to maintain control during an aborted landing after a tailwind encounter caused the accident.
