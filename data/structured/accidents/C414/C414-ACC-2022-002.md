# Narrative Summary

On June 18, 2022, the Cessna 414 landed at Pickens County Airport near Jasper, Georgia. The pilot reported three green landing gear indications before touchdown and described the landing as smooth and straight. After touchdown, the left main landing gear collapsed. The airplane skidded left, departed the runway surface, and struck a taxiway sign. The left wing sustained substantial damage. Examination found that the left main landing gear torque tube had fractured. NTSB materials examination identified multiple fatigue cracks near the weld between the boss arm and tube body. Cessna Multi-engine Service Bulletin MEB09-2 required replacement of the torque tube at 4,000 hours time in service, but the airplane had about 5,000 hours total time and the torque tube was original. The pilot was not injured.
