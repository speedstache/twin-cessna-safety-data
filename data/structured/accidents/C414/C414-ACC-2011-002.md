# Narrative Summary

On March 22, 2011, a Cessna 414A began a Part 135 nonscheduled passenger flight from Aberdeen Regional Airport in South Dakota.

Instrument meteorological conditions prevailed and the runway surface was reported as snow and ice covered.

During the takeoff roll, the pilot reported that a strong crosswind gust forced the airplane off the left side of the runway.

The airplane struck runway lights and the pilot maneuvered it back onto the runway before shutting down the engines.

A postaccident inspection found substantial damage from a puncture in the pressure vessel.

The pilot reported no preimpact mechanical malfunction or failure.

The NTSB determined that inadequate compensation for gusty crosswinds during takeoff resulted in loss of directional control.
