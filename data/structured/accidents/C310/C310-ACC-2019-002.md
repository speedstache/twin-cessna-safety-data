# Narrative Summary

On August 4, 2019, a Cessna 310I departed J Lynn Helms Sevier County Airport on a local Part 91 flight test in visual meteorological conditions.
Radar and GPS data showed the airplane maneuvering around the departure airport area at about 2,000 to 1,800 ft above ground level.
The airplane turned toward the airport and entered a descent that continued until ground contact.
During the descent, recorded airspeed decreased below the airplane’s published stall speed.
The airplane impacted a pasture less than one mile south of the airport.
The landing gear and flaps were found retracted.
Examination of the airframe and engines did not reveal anomalies that would have precluded normal operation.
Weather information showed cumuliform clouds and dissipating precipitation near the accident site, with scattered thunderstorm activity forecast in the area.
The NTSB determined that the loss of control occurred for reasons that could not be determined based on the available information.
