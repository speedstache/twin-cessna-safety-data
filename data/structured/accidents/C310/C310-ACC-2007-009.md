# Narrative Summary

On December 19, 2007, a Cessna 310P, N5811M, sustained substantial damage during a touch-and-go training flight at Dunnellon, Florida. The Part 91 instructional flight involved a certified flight instructor and a pilot receiving instruction practicing touch-and-go landings. After the second touchdown, the flaps were retracted and power was applied for takeoff. The airplane pulled to the right, and the instructor assumed control; however, directional control was lost and the right wing struck the runway before the airplane departed the right side of the runway. Examination revealed that new scissors bushings had been installed on the right main landing gear the previous day. The required wide area washers had been installed on the wrong side of the scissors, contrary to the maintenance manual. Following the second touch-and-go landing, the scissors separated and the right main landing gear collapsed. The NTSB determined the probable cause was improper installation of the right main landing gear scissors by maintenance personnel.
