# Narrative Summary

After a local flight, the pilot attempted to lower the landing gear but did not receive a down indication. Following troubleshooting and tower confirmation that the gear appeared down, the pilot landed, and the right main landing gear collapsed a few seconds after touchdown.

Examination found that the right main landing gear down-lock tension was far below the 40 to 60 pound range specified by the service manual. The fork bolt used to adjust the down-lock tension was covered in grime, indicating it had not been adjusted during recent inspections.

Investigators also found a failed drive tube and an intermittent down-limit indication issue. The report documented a prior right-main-gear anomaly in 2013 and stated that the full rigging procedure, including down-lock brace tension verification, had not been used during follow-up inspections.

The pilot had 3,350 hours total time and 1,650 hours in make and model. His flight review was current at the time of the accident.

The evidence supports maintenance-induced landing gear misrigging, compounded by incomplete follow-up troubleshooting after prior gear anomalies.
