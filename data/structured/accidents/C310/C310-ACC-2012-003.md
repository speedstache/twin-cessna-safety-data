# Narrative Summary

The pilots originally planned to test the recently maintained right engine in the traffic pattern before repositioning the airplane, but the flight was delayed by an adjustment to the propeller control friction lock. Because of the delay, they departed after sunset and elected to fly directly to an unlighted private airstrip.

The airplane had known airworthiness issues before departure. Witnesses reported that the nose gear strut was flat, the crew discussed leaving the landing gear extended, and the airplane did not have a current annual inspection.

While en route, the right engine began to lose oil pressure and then lost total power. The crew shut the engine down and feathered the propeller, but the airplane was unable to maintain altitude and collided with trees before impact fire consumed most of the airframe.

Postaccident examination found that the right engine oil filter adapter had not been assembled or secured in accordance with the manufacturer’s instructions. The mechanic later stated that he had not used the STC instructions or a torque wrench when reassembling and installing the adapter.

The NTSB found no mechanical malfunctions with the left engine that would have precluded normal operation. It concluded that the pilot failed to maintain control after shutting down the right engine following oil loss caused by improper maintenance.
