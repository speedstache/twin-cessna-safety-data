# Narrative Summary

On September 6, 2018, a Cessna T310R departed Laramie Regional Airport on a Part 91 business flight in visual meteorological conditions.
After climbing toward cruise altitude, the pilot began leaning the fuel mixture for cruise power.
The pilot felt vibration and fluttering, slowed the airplane, began troubleshooting, and turned back toward the airport.
When the pilot advanced the throttles to takeoff power, there was no response from the engines.
The pilot attempted to glide toward an interstate for an emergency landing but was unable to reach it.
The airplane made a gear-up forced landing in a field and sustained substantial damage.
The pilot reported that he was distracted by the vibration and noise.
The pilot reported that he failed to enrichen the fuel mixtures after beginning the leaning procedure.
The pilot reported no mechanical anomalies that would have precluded the engines from producing full power.
