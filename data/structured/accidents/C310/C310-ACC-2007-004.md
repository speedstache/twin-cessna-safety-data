# Narrative Summary

On April 26, 2007, a Cessna 310R, N3700G, operated under Part 135 as a non-scheduled air taxi flight, sustained substantial damage following a forced landing near Hatch, New Mexico. Approximately 20 minutes into the flight, the left engine lost power during cruise. The pilot reported that he did not switch fuel tanks. Air traffic control provided vectors to Hatch Municipal Airport; however, the pilot determined he could not reach the airport and elected to perform a forced landing in the desert. During the landing roll, the right wing struck high vegetation, crushing the right wing spar and separating the right tip tank. Postaccident examination revealed the auxiliary fuel tanks were empty and no mechanical anomalies were found with the airplane systems. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s inattentiveness to fuel flow and fuel selector valve position resulting in fuel starvation, with improper use of procedures and in-flight decision making as contributing factors.
