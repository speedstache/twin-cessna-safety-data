# Narrative Summary

The Part 135 non-scheduled passenger flight departed Key West for Marco Island in day VMC. Before takeoff, the pilot did not visually check the fuel tanks and relied on gauge readings and his fuel calculations. About 15 miles from the destination the right engine coughed and lost power, followed several minutes later by loss of power from the left engine. The pilot ditched the airplane in the Gulf of Mexico and occupants exited the aircraft; one passenger reported not being able to find a life vest. After recovery, 14 ounces of fuel were drained from the left main tank and no fuel was recovered from the right main tank, and testing found no preimpact failure of the fuel quantity indicating system or engines. Fuel records and postaccident calculations indicated the actual fuel load for the accident flight was far lower than the pilot reported and only supported about 34 minutes of operation at typical power settings. The NTSB determined the probable cause was inaccurate fuel consumption calculations resulting in fuel exhaustion.
