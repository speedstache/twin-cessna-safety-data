# Narrative Summary

Witnesses observed the airplane depart and then abruptly pitch nose down during the initial climb, continuing into a rapid descent to ground impact. The pilot was fatally injured and the airplane was destroyed.

Postaccident examination found that the elevator trim pushrod was attached to the trim tab but not attached to the trim tab actuator. The securing bolt, nut, washer, and cotter pin were missing, and the disconnected pushrod had jammed in a position that produced an abnormally large nose-down trim condition.

Manufacturer follow-on action included a service bulletin specifying replacement hardware, and the FAA later issued an airworthiness directive requiring compliance. Engine examinations found no preimpact anomalies that would have explained the loss of control.

The flight was operated under Part 91 as a personal cross-country flight in day visual conditions. The pilot reported 470 hours total time on his medical application, but no logbooks were recovered, so time in type and recency could not be established from the report.

Because the record does not identify an approved taxonomy factor that cleanly captures the missing hardware mechanism without overreaching beyond the report, the contributing factor was coded as unknown.
