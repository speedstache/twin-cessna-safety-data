# Narrative Summary

On August 23, 2025, the Cessna 310Q was on an IFR Part 91 personal flight from Fairfield County Airport, South Carolina, to Milledgeville, Georgia. Before the flight, the pilot had obtained an instrument flight rules equipment recertification. During the arrival, the pilot was cleared for the RNAV Runway 10 approach and acknowledged the instruction to cross the initial approach fix at or above 3,000 feet. There were no further transmissions from the pilot. About 10 seconds after the last communication, the airplane entered a left turn and descended. The controller issued a low altitude alert, and the airplane continued descending until flight track data was lost. Weather in the area included overcast clouds around 2,200 to 2,500 feet with cloud tops reported to about 10,000 feet. The airplane struck pine trees and terrain and was destroyed. Examination confirmed flight control continuity through overload breaks, and fuel system components that contained fluid showed aviation fuel without contamination. The report is preliminary and the wreckage was retained for further examination.
