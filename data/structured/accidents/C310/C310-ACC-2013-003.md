# Narrative Summary

The pilot was conducting a practice instrument approach in visual conditions when a hawk struck the right wing. The collision occurred at 3,000 feet msl while the airplane was outside the final approach fix.

The pilot maintained control of the airplane after the strike. He transitioned to a visual approach and completed a straight-in landing with no flaps.

The airplane landed without further incident. However, the bird strike caused substantial damage to the right wing.

The flight was conducted under Part 91 as a personal operation, and the weather at the accident site was clear with 10 miles visibility. No pilot injuries were reported.

The NTSB determined that the accident was caused by a bird strike during the practice instrument approach.
