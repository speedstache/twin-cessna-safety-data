# Narrative Summary

After an uneventful flight, smoke appeared from below the instrument panel as the airplane was parked on the ramp. The pilot shut down, evacuated the airplane, opened the nose access panel, and extinguished a fire in the nose compartment.

Postaccident examination found thermal damage centered around the cabin heater installation. Pressure testing identified multiple leaks in the heater assembly, including cracked and leaking weld areas.

Investigators also found evidence of repeated fuel leakage in the heater fuel pump box and a blocked heater drain line containing combustion byproducts. Maintenance records did not document the recurring heater and fuel line inspections recommended by manufacturer guidance.

The flight was operated under Part 91 as a personal flight in day visual conditions. The pilot had 1,300 hours total time and 30 hours in make and model.

The event was consistent with a heater-system fuel leak and fire on an aging airplane in which prior leakage indications and recurring inspection needs were not adequately addressed.
