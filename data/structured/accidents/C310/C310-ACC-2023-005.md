# Narrative Summary

On November 13, 2023, the Cessna 310D departed Hayden, Idaho, on a Part 91 personal flight. The pilot did not hold a multiengine rating. During climb, the pilot observed asymmetric manifold pressure and reported that the left engine throttle could not be reduced below 21 inches of manifold pressure. The pilot attempted troubleshooting and returned for a precautionary landing. During final approach, the airspeed decayed to about 80 mph. The airplane rolled left, the stall horn sounded, and the airplane descended into terrain. Examination of the engines did not reveal a preimpact mechanical failure that would have precluded operation. Examination of the left engine throttle cable installation found nonconforming hardware and an arrangement that could restrict throttle cable travel. Maintenance records were not available, and the report did not determine when the throttle cable modification had been performed.
