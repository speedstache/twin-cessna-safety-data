# Narrative Summary

On January 31, 2007, a Cessna 310Q, N20SS, sustained substantial damage following a landing gear collapse at Atlantic City International Airport, New Jersey. The Part 91 executive flight was conducted in day VMC. After lowering the landing gear on base leg, the pilot initially observed three green lights, followed shortly by an unsafe gear indication with the two main gear lights extinguished. The pilot recycled the landing gear and again received an unsafe indication. After troubleshooting, he manually extended the landing gear using the hand crank, and cranked it past the mechanical stop. The airplane landed with the left main landing gear in an over-center condition, and the gear collapsed during rollout. Examination revealed a fatigued and broken tab on the left main landing gear squat switch, which rendered the safe gear indication inoperative. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the inoperative safe gear indicator light switch, with the pilot’s improper hand-cranking of the gear contributing to the collapse.
