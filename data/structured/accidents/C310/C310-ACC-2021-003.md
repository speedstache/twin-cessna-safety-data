# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed on a personal flight in dark night visual meteorological conditions. ADS-B data showed the airplane climbed to about 200 feet, turned right, and descended into rising terrain northwest of the airport. The debris distribution was consistent with a wings-level, slightly nose-down impact. Postaccident examination of the airframe and engines revealed no mechanical anomalies that would have precluded normal operation. Autopsy findings indicated the pilot was actively having a heart attack in the hours before the accident. The NTSB determined that incapacitating effects of the myocardial infarction resulted in loss of control and impact with terrain.
