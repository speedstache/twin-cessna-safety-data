# Narrative Summary

During a local Part 91 aerial photography flight, the pilot climbed the Cessna 310Q to 13,500 ft mean sea level and flew for about 2 hours before returning to Bartow, Florida, for landing. While maneuvering in the traffic pattern, the right engine lost power and the left engine then also stopped producing power. The pilot determined that the airplane would not reach the runway and completed a forced landing in swampy terrain, substantially damaging the wings, fuselage, and tail.

Postaccident examination found both fuel selector handles in the auxiliary position. Several gallons of fuel were recovered from the left auxiliary and locker tanks, and a Shadin fuel flow indicator showed fuel remaining consistent with the reported flight duration. Examination of the airframe and both engines found no preimpact mechanical anomalies that would have prevented normal operation.

The Cessna owner's manual directed that both fuel selectors be moved to the main tanks before landing and stated that operation on auxiliary tanks below 1,000 ft agl was not recommended. The pilot reported 5,107 hours total time and 219 hours in make and model, with 13 hours flown in the previous 30 and 90 days.
