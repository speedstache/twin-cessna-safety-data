# Narrative Summary

The pilot was conducting touch-and-go landings during a post-annual-inspection maintenance test flight at El Monte Airport.

He reported that after touchdown he retracted the flaps and increased power, and the airplane then pulled to the right.

He applied full left rudder and reduced power to idle, but the airplane exited the runway onto soft ground and the right main landing gear collapsed.

Inspection of the airplane did not reveal engine or flight control abnormalities that would have precluded normal operation.

The pilot reported about 2,500 total hours of flight time and about 1,200 hours in the accident make and model, but only about 4 hours in the Cessna 310 during the previous year.
