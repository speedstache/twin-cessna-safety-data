# Narrative Summary

Shortly after departure, the pilot observed that the landing gear did not retract normally and then would not extend with a green light indication. After troubleshooting and obtaining a tower visual confirmation that the gear appeared down, he continued to land.

During the end of the landing roll, the left main landing gear retracted and the airplane sustained substantial damage to the left wing spar. The pilot and two passengers were not injured.

Postaccident examination found the landing gear compartment dirty and covered in oil and debris. Investigators were unable to determine the precise reason for the gear retraction.

The flight was operated under Part 91 as an instructional flight in day visual conditions. The pilot had 25,800 hours total time and 3,108 hours in make and model.

Because the initiating mechanical reason could not be established, the event was coded with an unknown contributing factor pending clearer evidence.
