# Narrative Summary

On January 1, 2011, a Cessna 310F impacted trees during a visual approach to runway 19 at Orange, Massachusetts.

The pilot decided at the last moment to perform a touch-and-go at the airport before returning to the departure point.

He reported seeing white and red lights near the runway and believed they were visual approach slope indicator lights, although the airport had no such system.

Published airport information cautioned about trees at the approach end of runway 19 and noted a displaced threshold.

The pilot did not review airport publications before the flight.

He did not hold a multiengine rating or multiengine solo endorsement, and his last logged night flight was in 2000.

The airplane struck trees about 640 feet before the displaced threshold area and came to rest inverted.
