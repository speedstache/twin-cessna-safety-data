# Narrative Summary

On March 11, 2011, a Cessna 310R impacted terrain after a rapid nose-down descent shortly after takeoff from Smyrna, Tennessee.

The flight was a maintenance acceptance test flight following installation of a new avionics suite and autopilot.

Previous test flights had identified a pitch-porpoise anomaly when the altitude hold feature was engaged.

A technician had advised waiting for additional parts and further troubleshooting before another flight.

The pilot departed alone on the second flight of the day.

The elevator trim actuator was found in a full nose-down position after the accident.

The pilot had been described by the technician as not fully understanding operation of the new system during earlier test flights.
