# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed on a cross-country flight after all four fuel tanks had been filled. Recorded EFIS data showed that fuel was initially drawn from the main tanks and later from the auxiliary tanks. The auxiliary tanks were exhausted after about 30 minutes of use, and fuel flow to both engines decreased. The pilot advised air traffic control of a loss of power in both engines and received vectors toward a diversion airport. The airplane impacted trees and terrain about one-quarter mile from the runway. Examination found no preimpact mechanical malfunctions or failures, and both fuel selectors were found positioned to the auxiliary tanks.
