# Narrative Summary

After flying over Yellowstone National Park, the pilot requested an IFR clearance direct to Billings and climbed to 15,000 feet. Shortly after the climb, radar showed the airplane turn east and enter a rapid descent before impacting mountainous terrain.

The airplane was destroyed and all four occupants were fatally injured. Wreckage examination indicated that the left engine was likely not operating at impact, while the right engine showed no disqualifying anomalies.

An AIRMET for icing was in effect between 14,000 feet and flight level 220 in the area. The investigation could not determine whether the pilot was aware of the icing advisory or the reason for the left-engine power loss.

The pilot held a commercial certificate and reported about 571 total hours on his recent medical application; operator records indicated 162.7 hours in the Cessna 310. The report did not state multiengine total time or recent flight activity.

Because the mechanism behind the engine power loss could not be determined, the contributing factor was coded as unknown despite the known loss of control following the power loss.
