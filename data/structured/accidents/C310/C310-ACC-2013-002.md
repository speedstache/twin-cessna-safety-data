# Narrative Summary

After takeoff, the pilot selected the landing gear up and heard an unusual banging sound from the right side of the airplane. The right main landing gear did not fully retract, and cycling the gear did not restore a down-and-locked indication.

The pilot attempted manual extension without success, declared an emergency, and returned for landing. A low pass was conducted so the tower could visually inspect the landing gear, and the airplane then landed.

During the landing roll, the right main landing gear collapsed and the airplane slid before coming to rest. The right horizontal stabilizer sustained substantial damage.

Postaccident examination found that the right main gear bracket assembly had separated from its wing attachment fitting. Investigators also found that the torque tube bearing would not rotate and lacked lubrication.

The NTSB concluded that the right main gear bracket assembly separated because the torque tube bearing was not properly lubricated, allowing wear to progress until the fitting could no longer support the load.
