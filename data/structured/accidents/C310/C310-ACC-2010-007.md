# Narrative Summary

The pilot reported that the flight was normal until the airplane was on final approach to Tripp Creek Airport in St. Johns, Michigan.

He stated that the airplane yawed slightly right and that he initiated a go-around at about 100 feet above ground level.

He reported a lack of engine power from the right engine, used power from the left engine to arrest the descent, and the airplane then impacted terrain adjacent to the runway.

Postaccident examination of the right engine was limited to confirming engine compression, which was reported as normal.

The pilot had 999 hours total time and 19 hours in the accident make and model.
