# Narrative Summary

Shortly after departure, the pilot requested a destination change and stated that he needed to shut down one engine. ADS-B data showed the airplane maneuvering toward the new destination airport at airspeeds near the airplane minimum controllable airspeed. Witness video showed the left propeller was not turning and the right propeller was producing power. The airplane rolled sharply left and impacted terrain, and a postcrash fire followed. Examination of the left engine revealed crankcase damage, a separated No. 2 connecting rod, and signatures consistent with loss of lubrication. Maintenance records showed prior work for a low oil pressure problem and later low cylinder compression readings.
