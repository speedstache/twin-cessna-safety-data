On December 6, 2006, a Cessna 310B, N6640B, was substantially damaged when the left main landing gear collapsed while taxiing from landing at Frontier Airpark (WN53), Lake Stevens, Washington. The flight was conducted under Part 91 as a personal flight in day VMC. The commercial pilot and one passenger were not injured. The airplane had departed Arlington, Washington, approximately 20 minutes earlier. While taxiing to the hangar, the left main landing gear collapsed and the airplane settled onto the left wing tip tank, resulting in structural damage to the left outboard wing rib and wrinkling of the upper wing skin. The landing gear had reportedly collapsed approximately six months prior to this accident. Examination did not determine a specific reason for the gear collapse. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the collapse of the main landing gear for an undetermined reason while taxiing from landing.
