# Narrative (5–10 factual sentences; no speculation)

On November 26, 2007, a Cessna 310R impacted terrain while conducting an ILS approach to runway 36 at Owensboro, Kentucky, in night IMC conditions. The Part 91 positioning flight was conducted under IFR after earlier weather delays. An AIRMET for icing was in effect and overcast conditions with mist were reported.

After intercepting the localizer and crossing the final approach fix, the aircraft descended on the approach path. Approximately 3 nautical miles beyond the final approach fix, radar data showed the aircraft initiating a climbing right turn. The pilot then transmitted that he tumbled his gyros. No further communications were received.

Radar data showed the aircraft reaching approximately 2,100 feet before descending. Witnesses observed the aircraft in a steep nose-down attitude prior to impact. Postaccident examination revealed no evidence of preimpact mechanical failures, including both vacuum pumps and gyro instruments, which exhibited rotational scoring consistent with operation at impact. The pilot held a commercial certificate with instrument rating and was current per regulatory requirements. The accident was determined to be a loss of control during approach for undetermined reasons.
