# Narrative Summary

After landing at Roswell International Air Center, the pilot taxied toward the parking area in day VMC conditions. During a left turn while taxiing, the right main landing gear collapsed and the airplane sustained substantial damage to the right wing and horizontal stabilizer. An FAA inspector determined that the landing gear was not properly reassembled after maintenance. A bushing was left out at the right main landing gear side link lower attach point. The incorrect reassembly reduced down-lock tension below allowable limits during subsequent landing gear cycles. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the airframe and powerplant mechanic’s incorrect reassembly of the landing gear.
