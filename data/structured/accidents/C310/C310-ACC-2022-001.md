# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed on the last of a multi-leg flight in night IMC conditions. The pilot deviated from the filed flight path to avoid weather enroute. During the flight the pilot reported the right engine running rough in cruise. At 2.2 hours into a planned 2 hour flight, both engines began "surging" and then stopped producing power. Pilot indicated that both fuel gauges read empty. 

The pilot completed a forced landing into a horse pasture where the aircraft impacted a fence, causing substantial damage to the wings and fuselage. 

The pilot stated that headwings and deviation on route caused the flight to be longer than planned and also indicated that the fuel gauges were not reliable. Pilot also indicated that there may have been a discrepancy between fuel requested and received at one stop, but the pilot did not obtain a receipt or verify amount of fuel dispensed. 

The cause of this incident was determined to be the pilot's inadequate fuel planning and inflight fuel management which resulted in fuel exhaustion to both engines.
