# Narrative Summary

The pilot was conducting an aerial survey flight and held for about 8 to 10 minutes with a quartering tailwind during taxi and run-up. Shortly after takeoff, the airplane climb rate decreased and acceleration stopped. Postaccident evidence showed the left fuel selector off, the left throttle near idle, the left propeller near feather, and rudder trim to the right, consistent with the left engine being partially secured. The airplane then banked left into the partially secured engine and entered a rapidly descending right turn. Toxicological testing by the state forensic science office showed elevated carboxyhemoglobin, and an exemplar test showed that exhaust could enter the cockpit under similar wind conditions. The NTSB determined that carbon monoxide impairment led to failure to maintain a controllable airspeed after partially securing an engine.
