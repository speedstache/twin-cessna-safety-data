# Narrative Summary

The airplane was on approach to Sierra Blanca Regional Airport at Ruidoso, New Mexico, when witnesses observed it high on final and then descending rapidly.

One surviving passenger reported that the wings were rocking and that the airplane felt like it was being tossed by the wind shortly before impact.

Wreckage examination showed impact in a left bank and steep nose-down attitude, and both engines showed evidence of operating at impact.

Postaccident examination did not reveal any anomalies that would have prevented normal airplane or engine operation.

The pilot had about 842 total flight hours, about 156 hours of multiengine time, and 5 hours in the accident make and model.
