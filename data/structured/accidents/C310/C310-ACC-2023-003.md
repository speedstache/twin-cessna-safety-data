# Narrative Summary

On May 25, 2023, the Cessna 310I was landing at Sunnyside Municipal Airport on a Part 91 personal flight. The pilot reported that the final approach was normal and that the landing gear indicated down. The landing was described as firm. Shortly after touchdown, the right main landing gear collapsed and the airplane veered off the runway. The airplane came to rest in a field about 100 feet from the runway centerline. The right horizontal stabilizer and elevator sustained substantial damage. Examination of the landing gear components found overload signatures in the side brace lock assembly and associated attachment hardware. The NTSB determined that the right main landing gear side brace lock assembly failed from overstress as a result of a hard landing.
