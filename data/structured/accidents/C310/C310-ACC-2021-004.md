# Narrative Summary

The airplane had not flown for about nine months and was being flown on a ferry permit to complete an overdue annual inspection. Before departure, the pilot returned to the ramp due to a right-engine miss and later reported that it was cleared up enough to try again. ADS-B data showed the airplane climbed to 5,800 feet mean sea level, turned left, and began a rapid descent. During the descent, the empennage and outboard wing sections separated. Postaccident examination found no evidence of mechanical malfunctions or failures that would have precluded normal operation, although impact damage prevented a functional flight-control examination. The NTSB determined that the loss of control occurred for reasons that could not be determined.
