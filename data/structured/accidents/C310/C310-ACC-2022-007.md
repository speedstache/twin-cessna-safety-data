# Narrative Summary

During approach, the pilot lowered the landing gear and only the nose gear light illuminated. After a missed approach, the pilot attempted manual gear extension but felt the system was not engaging correctly and continued turning the manual extension handle until it stopped. Tower controllers advised that the landing gear appeared down, and the landing gear warning horn did not activate. During landing rollout, the left main landing gear collapsed followed by the right main landing gear folding. Examination found that the landing gear actuator upper bellcrank had overtraveled beyond the down limit switch and reached the internal stop pin. The NTSB determined that the main landing gear collapsed due to actuator overtravel for undetermined reasons.
