# Narrative Summary

On March 22, 2018, a Cessna 310R sustained substantial damage when the right landing gear collapsed during landing rollout at Charles B. Wheeler Downtown Airport.
The Part 91 personal flight was operating in visual meteorological conditions on an instrument flight rules flight plan.
The airline transport pilot reported that the landing gear position lights were all green during approach and that the landing was normal.
A witness reported that the landing appeared normal and was not a hard landing.
During rollout, the right landing gear collapsed and the airplane skidded about 1,200 to 1,500 ft before veering off the runway.
The horizontal stabilizer and elevator sustained substantial damage.
Landing gear components were examined by the NTSB Materials Laboratory.
Several fracture features were consistent with overstress, and the end fitting bolt had separated through a thread root.
The exact cause of the end fitting bolt separation could not be determined due to smearing and secondary damage.
