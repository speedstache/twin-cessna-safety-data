# Narrative Summary

On August 27, 2023, the Cessna 310Q was landing at Santa Fe Regional Airport on a Part 91 personal flight. The pilot reported being high and fast on final approach. When power was reduced for landing, the airplane descended rapidly. The airplane landed hard, the left landing gear collapsed, and the airplane exited the runway. The left wing sustained substantial damage. The pilot reported no preimpact mechanical malfunctions or failures that would have precluded normal operation. The accident occurred in visual meteorological conditions at a high-elevation airport. The NTSB determined that an improper landing flare following an unstable approach resulted in the hard landing.
