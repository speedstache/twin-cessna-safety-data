# Narrative Summary

On January 10, 2007, a Cessna 310, N2625H, was destroyed following a loss of control while returning to Hawkins Field Airport, Jackson, Mississippi. The Part 91 personal cross-country flight had departed the same airport and was destined for Ocean Springs, Mississippi. Shortly after takeoff, the commercial pilot reported to air traffic control that he had found a problem and intended to return to the airport, but did not specify the nature of the issue. Radar data showed the airplane climbing to approximately 5,000 feet msl, initiating a 180-degree turn, and descending gradually toward the airport at groundspeeds between 160 and 170 knots. The airplane impacted terrain about 4 miles from the airport in a near-vertical, nose-down attitude and was consumed by a post-impact fire. Examination of the airframe, engines, and propellers revealed no pre-impact mechanical abnormalities. Both propellers showed rotational damage and were not feathered at impact. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s loss of control for undetermined reasons.
