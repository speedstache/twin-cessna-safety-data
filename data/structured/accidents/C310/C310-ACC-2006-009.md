A Cessna 310Q was conducting an ILS approach to Runway 19 at Waco Regional Airport at night in actual IMC under Part 91. Radar data showed the airplane was not stabilized during the final segment and flew groundspeeds below the published minimum approach speed of 89 knots. The aircraft decelerated further, lost altitude, and impacted terrain about 1.8 miles north of the runway, consistent with an inadvertent stall and loss of control. Weather at the airport was reported with a 200-foot overcast ceiling, mist/fog, and 1.5–2 miles visibility. Postaccident examination found no mechanical deficiencies that would have precluded normal operation. The pilot’s logbook indicated he was not current for flight in instrument meteorological conditions, and his FAA medical privileges had expired about eight months before the accident. Toxicology testing detected tetrahydrocannabinol and metabolites consistent with marijuana use, and also detected phentermine and promethazine. The NTSB determined the accident was caused by the pilot’s failure to maintain sufficient airspeed to avoid a stall during the instrument approach, with pilot impairment due to prohibited drugs as a causal factor.
