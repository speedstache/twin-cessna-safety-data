# Narrative Summary

The pilot landed without incident and was taxiing off the runway when the left main landing gear began to collapse. The airplane struck the ground on the left side, which resulted in substantial damage to the left wing and left horizontal stabilizer.

Examination showed that the left main gear bellcrank pin had separated from its bushing. The pin itself was not recovered.

Metallurgical examination found deformation and fracturing in the bellcrank bushing consistent with very large force applied along the axis of the link. The available parts showed overstress features, but investigators could not rule out other failure modes because the missing pin fragments were not available for analysis.

The airplane had accumulated about 3,675 total hours and about 25 hours since its most recent annual inspection, which had been completed about two months before the accident.

The NTSB determined that the left main landing gear collapsed due to failure of the bellcrank pin for undetermined reasons.
