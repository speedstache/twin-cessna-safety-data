# Narrative Summary
During visual conditions in a common practice area south of Long Beach, a Cessna 310P and a Cessna 172N converged at approximately 3,000 feet msl. Radar data depicted the 310P traveling eastbound while the 172 maneuvered on a southerly heading. A witness observed both aircraft at similar altitudes prior to collision. The aircraft collided nearly perpendicular to one another and impacted the ocean. Examination of wreckage revealed paint transfer consistent with the collision geometry. No preimpact mechanical anomalies were identified.
