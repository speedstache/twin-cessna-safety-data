# Narrative Summary

The pilot reported that during the landing roll of his second uneventful landing, the right main landing gear began to shimmy. He slowed the airplane and continued toward the nearest taxiway.

Before exiting the runway, the pilot intended to retract the flaps but inadvertently raised the landing gear handle instead. He quickly returned the handle to the down position, but the right main gear had already started to retract.

The airplane veered to the right, exited the runway surface, and came to rest on the grass. The right wing sustained substantial damage.

The pilot reported no preimpact mechanical malfunction with the airframe or engines that would have prevented normal operation. The event occurred in visual conditions during a local personal flight.

The NTSB determined that the landing gear collapsed because the pilot inadvertently retracted the gear instead of the flaps during the landing roll.
