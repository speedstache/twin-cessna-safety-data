# Narrative Summary

On June 27, 2024, the Cessna 310R was being operated on a Part 91 personal flight near Duluth, Minnesota. The pilot reported that the purpose of the flight was to test adjustments made to onboard avionics equipment. The airplane departed Superior, Wisconsin, flew to Duluth, and completed the runway 9 ILS approach. After stopping on the runway as instructed by air traffic control, the pilot received takeoff clearance to return to Superior. Seconds after rotation, the airplane rolled left. The pilot attempted to correct with aileron and rudder inputs. The airplane settled to the ground in a near-level attitude north of runway 9. The nose landing gear and right main landing gear collapsed, and the right wing and fuselage sustained substantial damage. The report is preliminary and does not identify a cause for the roll after takeoff.
