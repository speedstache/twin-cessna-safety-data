# Narrative Summary

During cruise flight under instrument meteorological conditions, the pilot reported a loss of manifold pressure in the left engine while at 16,000 feet mean sea level (msl). The pilot requested a descent and was cleared by air traffic control to 14,000 feet msl. Shortly thereafter, the pilot acknowledged a frequency change; no further communications were received.

Radar contact was lost at an encoded altitude of 11,400 feet msl. The aircraft impacted mountainous terrain at approximately 9,350 feet msl, and a post-impact fire ensued.

Weather conditions in the area included AIRMETs for instrument flight rules conditions, mountain obscuration, turbulence, and icing. Pilot reports in the region indicated light to moderate icing and turbulence at altitudes similar to the aircraft’s operating altitude.

Examination of the airframe and engines revealed no preimpact mechanical anomalies. The left propeller exhibited signatures consistent with a near-feathered condition at impact, while the right propeller displayed evidence of power.

The aircraft had received recent avionics upgrades three days prior to the accident.
