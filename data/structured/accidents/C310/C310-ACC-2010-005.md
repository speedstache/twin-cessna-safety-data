# Narrative Summary

During cruise flight near LaGrange, Indiana, the pilot reported a sudden vibration, decreased controllability, and a loss of altitude.

He shut down and feathered the left engine, but the vibration and altitude loss continued.

The pilot performed an off-airport landing to a pasture where the airplane struck a wire fence and a horse, and a postcrash fire ensued.

Inspection of the airframe did not reveal anomalies that would have induced a vibration, and test running of the right engine did not identify abnormalities that would have precluded normal operation.

The report noted that the airplane was capable of positive single-engine climb performance at the conditions present at the time of the accident.
