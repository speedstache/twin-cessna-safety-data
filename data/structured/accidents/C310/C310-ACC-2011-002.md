# Narrative Summary

On February 26, 2011, a Cessna 310K collided with trees and terrain during an instrument approach to Angleton, Texas.

The pilot had requested and been cleared for the RNAV GPS runway 17 approach at night.

Radar data showed the airplane intercepted the inbound course and descended to about 800 feet above ground level before radar contact was lost.

The wreckage was found in a wooded area about 1 mile east of the inbound course and 2.6 miles from the airport.

Postaccident examination of the airframe and both engines found no anomalies that would have precluded normal operation.

The pilot survived with serious injuries and later reported no recollection of the accident.

The flight was operated under Part 91 on a personal flight.
