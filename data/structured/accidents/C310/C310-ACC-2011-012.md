# Narrative Summary

On November 25, 2011, a Cessna 310I sustained substantial damage when the right main landing gear collapsed during landing rollout at Sedona, Arizona.

The pilot reported that the flight and landing were uneventful until the collapse occurred during the roll.

The airplane departed the right side of the runway, and the nose and left main landing gear also collapsed.

Postaccident examination showed impact damage to the right main inboard gear door consistent with the door being partially open.

Because those doors are closed when the gear is fully down, the damage indicated the right main gear was not down and locked at touchdown.

No preimpact broken or disconnected parts were found in the right main landing gear extension and retraction system.

Impact damage prevented a full gear extension and retraction test, so the initiating reason for the collapse could not be determined.
