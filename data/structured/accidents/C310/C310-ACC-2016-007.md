# Narrative Summary

The private pilot landed a Cessna 310R at Schafer Meadows, Montana, on an uphill grass runway during a Part 91 personal flight. After touchdown, the airplane veered slightly to one side, then the other, before exiting the left side of the runway. The landing gear collapsed and the stabilizer and elevator were substantially damaged.

Postaccident examination found the left landing gear upper trunnion fracture separated. Investigators could not determine whether the trunnion fracture caused the veering or whether it fractured during the landing gear collapse. The accident therefore remained a loss of directional control during landing for reasons that could not be determined.

The runway was 3,200 ft long, turf, and located at an elevation of 4,856 ft. Reported weather was visual meteorological conditions. The pilot held a private certificate and the report listed about 1 hour total time and 1 hour in the accident make and model.
