# Narrative Summary

During night cruise flight at 8,000 feet mean sea level, the airplane ran out of fuel. The pilot executed a forced landing to a field, and the airplane sustained substantial damage.

The pilot reported no preaccident mechanical malfunctions or failures that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB attributed the event to inadequate preflight planning and fuel management.

The flight was operated under Part 91 as a personal flight. Weather at the accident site was visual with clear skies and mist reported nearby.

The pilot held a private certificate and had 398 hours total time, with 92 hours in make and model. No multiengine total was stated in the report.

The accident sequence was consistent with fuel exhaustion following deficient preflight and en route fuel management.
