# Narrative Summary

After an uneventful flight the pilot extended the landing gear in preparation for landing on runway 32 at an airport in Destin, FL. The pilot reported a loud "thump" sound under the cockpit followed by the main landing gear lights going out. The pilot continued the approach and did not re-cycle the gear to avoid damage. 

On landing, the right main landing gear light came back on but the left remained unlit. The left gear leg collapsed on rollout resulting in the left prop striking the runway and the aircraft rolling to a stop off the runway to the left. 

Probable cause for this incident was determined to be a bent down limit switch in the left gear which was found bent in the up position, causing the gear box to allow over-travel and retraction of the gear.
