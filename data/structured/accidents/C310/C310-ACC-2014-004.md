# Narrative Summary

During takeoff after a touch-and-go, the pilot noted that the left engine was not producing full power. After liftoff, the airplane would not accelerate or climb, and the pilot reduced power and made a forced landing.

The airplane sustained substantial damage during the landing sequence. Examination found that the left engine throttle cable had broken near the engine connection end.

Fracture examination showed fatigue features in the individual cable strands. Investigators also identified a shallow swage at the cable connection, but they could not determine whether that condition resulted from manufacturing or long-term deterioration.

The flight was a Part 91 proficiency flight in day visual conditions. The pilot had an estimated 8,000 hours total time and 83.3 hours in make and model.

Previous maintenance history for the throttle cable could not be established because earlier maintenance records were unavailable. The event was consistent with an age-related throttle cable failure resulting in partial power loss during takeoff.
