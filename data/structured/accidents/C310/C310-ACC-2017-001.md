# Narrative Summary

On April 8, 2017, a Cessna 310R was landing at Lakeland Linder Regional Airport in visual meteorological conditions after a flight from North Perry Airport.
The commercial pilot reported that he confirmed the landing gear was down and locked before landing.
After touchdown, the left wing began to sink and the airplane veered left off the runway onto grass.
The pilot shut down the left engine and then the right engine after the airplane departed the runway.
The left wing, left horizontal stabilizer, and left propeller assembly sustained substantial damage.
Examination of the left main landing gear found that the fork bolt was ripped out of the torque tube, damaging the tube and preventing a complete retraction test.
Visual examination noted that the over-centering tension on the down-lock assembly did not appear to be rigged correctly.
The maintenance logbook review did not identify recent maintenance to the main landing gear assembly.
The NTSB determined that the reason for the left main landing gear collapse could not be determined due to accident damage.
