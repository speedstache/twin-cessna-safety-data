A Cessna 310Q departed Orange County Airport (MGJ), Montgomery, New York, on a personal Part 91 flight in day VMC. The flight was the first flight after the aircraft completed an annual inspection six days earlier. Shortly after takeoff, the airplane’s pitch oscillated while climbing to about 200 to 500 feet AGL. The airplane then turned left, descended uncontrolled, and impacted trees upright about 1 mile southeast of the airport. The commercial pilot, the sole occupant, was fatally injured and the aircraft sustained substantial damage. Maintenance records and mechanic statements showed the elevator trim actuator had been removed and replaced during the annual inspection, and a mechanic with inspection authorization endorsed the return to service. Postaccident examination found the elevator trim cables were crossed between the trim actuator and the empennage pulley, contrary to the illustrated parts configuration. The elevator trim tab was found in the full (10-degree) nose-down position. The NTSB determined the accident resulted from improper maintenance and failure to verify the maintenance work, leading to a reverse elevator trim system and loss of control during initial climb.
