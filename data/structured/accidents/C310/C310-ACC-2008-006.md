# Narrative Summary

The pilot conducted a night VFR cross-country flight with ATC flight following from Iowa to Lake Placid, New York. About two hours from arrival, the pilot requested a briefing from flight service but was not provided the NOTAM about a closed runway at the destination. On approach the pilot activated the runway lights and landed on runway 14 without noticing abnormalities. During the landing roll the airplane encountered gaps where runway sections had been removed and sustained substantial damage to the landing gear and fuselage. An FAA inspector noted the runway was closed and under construction, with unlit X markings, flashing markers, and cones at the approach ends, while the runway lighting system was not deactivated. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s failure to observe the ongoing construction to the closed runway while landing, with flight service’s failure to brief the pilot as a contributing factor.
