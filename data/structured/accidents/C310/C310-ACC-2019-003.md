# Narrative Summary

On August 21, 2019, a Cessna 310L was on approach to Tampa, Florida, when the pilot observed that the right main landing gear would not indicate down and locked.
The pilot wiggled the light and changed the bulb, but the right main landing gear still did not indicate down and locked.
During touchdown, the right main landing gear began to collapse, and the pilot performed a go-around.
The pilot climbed to altitude and attempted gear cycling, emergency extension procedures, and abrupt maneuvers without getting the right main gear down and locked.
After a second landing attempt produced the same result, the pilot declared an emergency and diverted to a tower-controlled airport.
Tower personnel reported that the right main gear appeared to be down during a fly-by.
The pilot again felt the right main gear collapsing during landing and then elected to perform a gear-up landing.
During recovery, personnel could not lock the right main landing gear using the emergency system, although they manually pushed it over-center and braced it.
A complete examination of the right main landing gear system was not performed, and the reason for the anomaly could not be determined.
