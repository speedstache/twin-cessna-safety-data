# Narrative Summary

The pilot and copilot conducted a local Part 91 personal flight in a Cessna 310L from Linden, New Jersey, to perform a functional check of the airplane. On approach for landing, the pilot selected the landing gear, observed three green lights, and visually confirmed the nose gear was down. At touchdown, the crew heard a noise or bang, after which the tail dropped and the airplane departed the right side of the runway and settled in grass.

Recovery examination found the nose gear down and locked, but both main landing gears were partially collapsed and would not lock when moved by hand. Further inspection showed that the main landing gear actuator had traveled beyond the down-limit switch and toward the actuator's internal stop pin, which released the gear from its down-and-locked position. The reason for the actuator overtravel could not be determined because the wreckage was sold and sectioned before a more detailed examination could be completed.

The pilot held a commercial certificate and mechanic certificate and reported 1,660 hours total time, including 100 hours in make and model. Weather at the time of the accident was visual meteorological conditions.
