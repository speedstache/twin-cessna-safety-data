# Narrative Summary

On August 17, 2011, a Cessna 310Q crashed during the initial takeoff climb from Tupelo, Mississippi.

The airplane had just undergone an annual inspection during which the left engine number two cylinder was replaced.

To perform that work, the B-nut connecting the fuel supply hose to the manifold valve was removed and reinstalled.

Witnesses reported a loss of engine power at about 300 to 500 feet above the runway followed by a left turn and descent.

Postaccident examination found the B-nut on the left engine backed off about a quarter turn.

Engine testing showed that loosening the B-nut by slightly over a quarter turn caused immediate power loss.

Propeller evidence indicated the left propeller was at low power or windmilling and the right propeller was producing high power at impact.
