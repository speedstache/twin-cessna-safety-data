# Narrative Summary

During a night instrument approach to runway 9R at Pontiac, the airplane descended below the glidepath and impacted trees and terrain about 1,500 feet short of the runway. The pilot was fatally injured and the airplane was destroyed.

Weather at the time included fog, 1/4-mile visibility, a 200-foot vertical visibility, and runway visual range of 2,000 feet. Radar data showed an unstabilized final approach in speed and position along the glidepath.

The flight was operated as a Part 91 positioning flight by a Part 135 cargo operator. The pilot had 1,908 hours total time, about 422 hours multiengine time, and about 41 hours in the Cessna 310R.

The investigation found that much of the pilot's make-and-model training was provided outside the operator's approved Part 135 training structure. Company records also did not support that the required training method and minimum make-and-model training times had been met.

No mechanical anomalies were found that would have precluded normal operation. The event was consistent with CFIT during a night IMC approach with limited make-and-model experience and incomplete transition training.
