# Narrative Summary

The pilot completed three takeoffs and landings and then practiced in-flight maneuvers before returning to land. During the landing roll, the left wing dropped and the propellers and wingtip struck the runway after the left main gear collapsed.

Postaccident examination found that the lower adjusting bolt on the left main landing gear had fractured in overstress. The annual inspection had been completed the same day as the accident, and the left and right main landing gear side brace bolts and bushings had been replaced.

A manufacturer representative stated that the fracture area is one of the principal locations associated with improper down-lock adjustment. However, the investigation could not determine whether the fracture resulted from landing loads, improper adjustment, or a combination of both.

The pilot held a commercial certificate with 1,071 hours total time and 199 hours in make and model. His last flight review was dated December 14, 2013.

Because the report does not support a single approved taxonomy factor with adequate certainty, the contributing factor was coded as unknown despite the clear recent maintenance context.
