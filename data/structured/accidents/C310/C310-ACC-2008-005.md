# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed on a Part 91 personal flight in day VMC and the airplane later lost control during cruise near Latrobe, Pennsylvania. A witness observed the airplane flying low with the left wing up and right wing down, engines sounding like full power, and descending rapidly before impact. The airplane impacted wooded terrain and was destroyed by impact forces and a post-crash fire. Examination of the airplane revealed no evidence of preimpact mechanical failure or malfunction. The pilot did not hold a current FAA medical certificate and had been hospitalized for congestive heart failure several months before the accident. The pilot’s cardiologist stated the pilot should not have been flying with his medical condition. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s impairment/incapacitation (cardiovascular) resulting in inability to control the airplane.
