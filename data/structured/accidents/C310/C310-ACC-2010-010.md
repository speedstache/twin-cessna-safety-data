# Narrative Summary

During approach to Great Falls, the pilot selected the landing gear down but did not receive a green down light for the right main gear, and the in-transit light remained illuminated.

He checked the bulb, concluded that the indication problem was likely a bad switch, and completed an uneventful landing and rollout.

As the airplane turned off the runway at taxi speed, the right main gear folded back and the right wing contacted the taxiway.

Postaccident inspection found that the right main gear upper side link had not moved fully to its locked position and that the right main gear bellcrank failed in overload during the turn.

The investigation could not determine why the landing gear activation linkage was sufficiently out of adjustment to prevent full lock.
