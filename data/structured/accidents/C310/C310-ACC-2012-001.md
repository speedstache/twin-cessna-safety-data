# Narrative Summary

Shortly after the left engine was started on the ramp, the pilot observed smoke coming from the left engine nacelle and then from the area of the rudder pedals. He shut down both engines and exited the airplane before the left engine became engulfed in flames.

Local personnel extinguished the fire. The airplane sustained substantial fire damage to the left engine and the forward wing area.

Follow-up examination found the most intense heat damage centered around the left engine fuel strainer on the firewall. Battery and starter solenoids were located nearby.

Investigators did not pressurize the fuel system because of the extent of the fire damage. Review of the records found no recent maintenance in the area of the fuel strainer.

The NTSB concluded that fuel leaking from the left engine fuel strainer contacted adjacent electrical connections after engine start, resulting in the fire.
