# Narrative Summary

While landing at night in visual conditions, the airplane landed hard and the left main landing gear collapsed. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the fuselage.

The pilot reported no mechanical deficiencies that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB attributed the event to an improper landing flare.

The accident airport had a published note that its runway edge and end lights were not standard in size, spacing, and number. The pilot stated that he was accustomed to approaches with instrument and visual glidepath guidance.

The flight was operated under Part 91 as a personal flight. The pilot had about 25,000 hours total time and 15,000 hours in make and model.

The available evidence supports a hard-landing outcome, but it does not map cleanly to a more specific approved contributing factor in the current taxonomy.
