## Narrative

On April 2, 2006, at 2016 local time, a Cessna 310N experienced a nose landing gear collapse during the landing roll at Lincoln Regional Airport, Lincoln, California. The student pilot was operating under Part 91 in night visual meteorological conditions. The pilot reported hearing a bumping sound while transitioning from landing to taxi, followed by the collapse of the nose gear. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the nose fuselage structure after contacting the runway surface. Examination of the nose gear revealed that the drive tube was bent approximately 90 degrees inboard. An A&P mechanic determined that the nose gear over-center adjustment was out of tolerance, allowing the gear to unlock from the down position following impact. There was no evidence of mechanical failure or foreign object damage. The most recent annual inspection had been completed in May 2005.
