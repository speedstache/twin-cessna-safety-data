# Narrative Summary

The airplane was being operated on an aerial observation flight when it landed normally at Bakersfield. Immediately after touchdown, the right main landing gear began to wobble and then folded under the fuselage.

The pilot attempted to maintain directional control with left rudder and aileron, but the airplane veered off the runway and came to rest in adjacent dirt. The right wheel and lower strut had separated from the upper strut and were found on the runway.

A Federal Aviation Administration inspector found that the right main landing gear torque links had separated at the center hinge point. Damage to the surrounding components indicated a mechanical malfunction in the link assembly.

The wheel well area, including ribs near the gear attach point, was damaged during the collapse sequence. The most recent annual inspection had been completed about two weeks before the event, and 37 hours had accumulated since that inspection.

The NTSB concluded that failure of the right main landing gear link assembly caused the gear collapse during landing.
