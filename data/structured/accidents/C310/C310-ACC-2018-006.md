# Narrative Summary

On November 9, 2018, a Cessna 310R was operating on an instrument flight rules personal flight from Niagara Falls, New York, to Teterboro, New Jersey.
About 55 minutes into the flight, the pilot advised air traffic control that he needed to get on the ground immediately.
No further intelligible radio transmissions were received from the pilot.
A witness reported hearing engines revving up and down, seeing gray smoke trailing the airplane, and seeing a red-orange glow from under the right wing area.
Radar data showed the airplane enter a turning descent before radar contact was lost.
The wreckage distribution was consistent with a high-speed impact, and the pilot was fatally injured.
Autopsy findings were consistent with smoke or soot inhalation before impact, and the wreckage showed evidence of an in-flight fire.
The origin of the in-flight fire could not be determined.
The report also found that severe icing conditions during the final minutes of flight contributed to loss of airplane control.
