# Narrative Summary

On August 3, 2007, a Cessna 310Q, N7749Q, sustained substantial damage when it impacted construction equipment during a precautionary landing on a closed section of highway near Brownsburg, Indiana. The Part 91 personal flight was operating in day VMC under an IFR flight plan that was cancelled shortly before landing. While approaching Eagle Creek Airpark, the pilot reported that the right engine began to sputter and lose power. Rather than continuing to the airport, the pilot elected to land on a nearby highway that was closed for construction. During the landing roll, the right wing tip tank contacted a road grader, resulting in substantial damage to the tip tank, wing ribs, and aileron. Inspection of the airplane did not reveal any mechanical malfunction of the engine or airframe. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s poor in-flight planning for an off-airport precautionary landing and failure to maintain clearance from construction equipment during the landing roll.
