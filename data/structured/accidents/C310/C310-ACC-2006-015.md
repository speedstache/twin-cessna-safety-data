On January 15, 2006, a Cessna 310Q, N141DE, was substantially damaged during a landing roll at Griffith, Indiana. The Part 91 instructional flight involved a certified flight instructor and a student practicing touch-and-go landings. During the seventh landing, while approximately three-quarters down the runway at about 20 mph, the right main landing gear collapsed. The airplane slid off the right side of the runway and came to rest in the grass. The student reported that during the rollout the landing gear warning horn sounded and the gear down-and-locked light flickered prior to the collapse. A metallurgical examination of the right main gear down-lock link revealed fracture characteristics consistent with overload failure. The NTSB determined the probable cause was an overload failure of the right main landing gear locking mechanism due to undetermined reasons.
