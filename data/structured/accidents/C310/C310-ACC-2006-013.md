On May 10, 2006, a Cessna 310C, N1788H, was substantially damaged during landing at Queen City Municipal Airport, Allentown, Pennsylvania. The Part 91 personal flight was conducted in day VMC. The commercial pilot and one passenger were not injured. The pilot performed an initial approach and executed a go-around due to traffic. During the go-around, he retracted the landing gear and continued in the traffic pattern for another landing attempt. During the second landing, the pilot neglected to lower the landing gear and the underside of the airplane contacted the runway surface. The pilot reported no mechanical deficiencies, and an FAA inspector’s examination revealed no mechanical malfunctions. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s failure to lower the landing gear during landing.
