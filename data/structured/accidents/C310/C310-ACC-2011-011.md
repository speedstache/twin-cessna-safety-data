# Narrative Summary

On October 7, 2011, a Cessna T310R ditched in the Pacific Ocean near Hilo, Hawaii, after fuel exhaustion.

The pilot had departed on a transpacific ferry flight with about 393 gallons of fuel, which he calculated would provide about 14 hours of endurance.

About 9 hours after takeoff, he determined that changed winds would prevent reaching the destination.

He attempted to extend range by reducing fuel consumption but could not reach the airport.

The engine lost power about 12 miles from the airport and the pilot ditched the airplane.

The pilot was rescued and the airplane later sank.

The total flight duration was about 13 hours.
