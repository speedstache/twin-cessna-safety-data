# Narrative Summary

On December 21, 2007, a Cessna 310, N28836, collided with mountainous terrain near Glenwood, New Mexico, during a Part 91 personal cross-country flight. The non-certificated pilot and passenger were fatally injured, and the airplane was destroyed. The wreckage was located in the Gila Wilderness approximately 711 nautical miles from the last confirmed sighting. Evidence indicated the airplane had been refueled en route in Borger, Texas earlier that day. Weather analysis showed a strong cold front in the region with patchy IFR/MVFR conditions, mountain obscuration, and an AIRMET for moderate turbulence. The high terrain near the accident site, approximately 10,000 feet elevation, was likely obscured at the time of the accident. The pilot’s commercial and instructor certificates had been revoked, and his last FAA medical examination was in 1995. The NTSB determined the probable cause was a loss of control for undetermined reasons, with marginal weather conditions contributing.
