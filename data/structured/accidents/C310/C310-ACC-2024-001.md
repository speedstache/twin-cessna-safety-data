# Narrative Summary

On January 14, 2024, the Cessna 310R was on an IFR Part 91 personal flight from Carrizo Springs, Texas, to Bridgeport, Texas. The pilot obtained a weather briefing that included AIRMETs for icing along the route. During descent, the airplane likely passed through instrument meteorological conditions with moderate or greater icing potential. Air traffic control attempted to contact the pilot for several minutes, including low altitude alerts, but received no response. Flight track data showed a controlled descent on a relatively straight heading until impact. The airplane impacted terrain in a relatively level attitude and was destroyed by impact forces and postimpact fire. The wreckage examination found no evidence of a preimpact flight control or engine anomaly that would have precluded normal operation. Medical evidence showed cardiovascular disease and toxicology findings, but the NTSB could not determine the reason for incapacitation. The NTSB determined that pilot incapacitation for undetermined reasons resulted in controlled descent into terrain.
