# Narrative Summary

The pilot was landing at a non-towered airport at night to drop off a passenger. During the flare, just before touchdown, he saw three deer run onto the runway from his peripheral vision.

One deer was struck by the left main landing gear. The impact folded the left main gear aft as the airplane touched down.

The left wing then contacted the runway, and the airplane slid down the runway before veering off the left side and coming to rest. Substantial damage was found to the left wing and aileron.

The weather was visual and the runway surface was dry. No pilot or passenger injuries were reported.

The NTSB determined that the accident resulted from an inadvertent collision with a deer during landing.
