# Narrative Summary

The noninstrument-rated private pilot departed on a cross-country flight without obtaining a recorded weather briefing and without communicating with air traffic control during the flight. Existing advisories for IFR conditions had already been issued along the intended route.

Witnesses near the accident site reported overcast skies, fog, drizzle, and windy weather. They heard sounds consistent with an airplane circling, followed by a rapid descent and impact.

Weather observations and satellite imagery showed an overcast layer near the accident site with bases about 800 feet agl and tops near 9,500 feet msl. The investigation found that the pilot likely encountered instrument meteorological conditions and then lost control of the airplane.

The pilot had about 410 total flight hours, 16 hours in multiengine airplanes, and 16 hours in the accident make and model. He had no instrument rating and only limited simulated instrument time documented during earlier private pilot training.

The airplane was destroyed by impact forces, and both occupants were fatally injured. The NTSB concluded that the pilot’s decision to continue VFR flight into IMC, along with the failure to obtain a weather briefing, led to the loss of control.
