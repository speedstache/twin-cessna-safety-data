# Narrative Summary

The instructional flight departed Meadow Lake Airport for a local flight review in a Cessna 310C.

Radar data showed the airplane maneuvering in the practice area east of the airport before radar contact was lost.

The airplane impacted terrain near Calhan, Colorado, in a wings-level attitude that was consistent with a flat spin.

Examination of the airplane, related systems, and both engines revealed no anomalies that would have precluded normal operation.

Both the commercial pilot and the flight instructor held multiengine ratings and had substantial total flight time.
