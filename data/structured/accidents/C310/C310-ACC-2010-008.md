# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed Palo Alto in near-zero visibility instrument meteorological conditions on an IFR flight plan.

Air traffic control instructed the pilot to turn right to a heading of 060 degrees and climb to 3,000 feet, and also advised that takeoff was at the pilot's own risk because the runway was not visible from the tower.

Witnesses and recorded data indicated that the airplane emerged from the fog at low altitude and struck a power pole and power lines shortly after takeoff.

Examination of the airframe, engines, and propellers did not reveal any preimpact mechanical anomalies, and acoustic analysis indicated both engines were operating near full power.

Reported weather near the time of departure included fog, visibility as low as 1/8 mile, and a vertical visibility of 100 feet agl.
