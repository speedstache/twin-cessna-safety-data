# Narrative Summary

On April 1, 2025, the Cessna 310Q departed New Philadelphia, Ohio, on a Part 91 personal flight. Family members stated that the pilot had been practicing 360° maneuvers. Preliminary ADS-B data showed the airplane made a series of 360° turns southeast of the airport. Pilots in the traffic pattern heard the accident pilot announce a 5-mile final for runway 33, then announce that he was breaking off the approach to the east. Shortly after, the accident pilot announced that he was performing a 360° turn to enter final approach. The traffic-pattern pilots then observed the airplane in a nose-down attitude spinning toward the ground. A witness near the accident site observed a steep right bank followed by a spiraling nosedive. Postaccident examination established flight control continuity and crankshaft continuity on both engines. The report is preliminary and the wreckage was retained for further examination.
