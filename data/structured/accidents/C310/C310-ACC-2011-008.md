# Narrative Summary

On April 1, 2011, a Cessna 310F sustained substantial damage when the nose gear collapsed during landing rollout at Sanford, Florida.

The flight instructor and private pilot were conducting touch-and-go landings.

After gear retraction on departure, they heard a loud bang but continued the flight.

On the final landing, the gear-down light did not illuminate, though a visual tower check indicated all three gears appeared down.

During touchdown on the full-stop landing, the nose gear collapsed.

Postaccident examination found the nose gear strut reservoir had no air pressure and very low fluid.

The underserviced condition caused binding in the nose gear fork assembly and failure of a nose gear push rod.
