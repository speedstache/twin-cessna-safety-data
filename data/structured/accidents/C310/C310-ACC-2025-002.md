# Narrative Summary

On April 11, 2025, the Cessna 310R departed Boca Raton, Florida, on a Part 91 personal flight. The accident flight was the first flight after completion of the airplane’s annual inspection. Surveillance video showed the airplane maintained runway centerline during the takeoff roll. Shortly after rotation, the airplane yawed left and continued in a left turn. Witness videos showed the airplane in a left yaw at low altitude, and audio was consistent with both engines operating. Preliminary ADS-B data showed a left drift, a left 180° turn, and multiple left 360° turns. One pilot reported to air traffic control that there was a problem with the rudder and that only left turns could be made. The airplane impacted trees, a road, and railroad tracks, and was destroyed by impact and postimpact fire. The preliminary examination documented rudder control continuity findings, rudder trim position, and impact-related cable separations, but the initiating failure had not been determined.
