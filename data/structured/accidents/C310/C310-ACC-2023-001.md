# Narrative Summary

On May 6, 2023, the Cessna 310D was on a Part 91 personal flight from Houston, Texas, to Ennis, Texas. During final approach, the pilot initiated a go-around for a reason not determined in the report. The airplane veered right, rolled right, impacted trees and terrain, and was destroyed by a postimpact fire. The right engine initially ran roughly during postaccident testing. The right engine fuel plug and screen assembly was obstructed by black debris, string-like fibers, and metallic particles. The NTSB materials examination identified polyurethane debris consistent with flexible fluid-carrying hose material. Maintenance records did not show that the flexible hoses had been replaced since 2001. The report also noted that records did not indicate the fuel plug and screen had been cleaned during the August 2022 annual engine inspection as recommended. The NTSB determined that reduced fuel flow from fuel screen contamination caused a partial loss of right engine power.
