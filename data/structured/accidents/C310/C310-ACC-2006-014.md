On April 12, 2006, a Cessna 310K, N6981L, was substantially damaged during landing at Brooksville, Florida. The Part 91 personal flight was conducted in day VMC. The private pilot and three passengers were not injured. During the landing rollout, the airplane began pulling to the right despite full rudder input. The airplane departed the right side of the runway, entered the grass, and impacted orange trees. Postaccident examination by an FAA inspector revealed that the right main landing gear had separated from the airplane. Inspection of the forward trunnion assembly found that one of the required u-bolts was missing and the pivot piece was not properly secured. Maintenance records indicated the right main landing gear had been recently repaired, and the airplane had accumulated about 9 hours since that maintenance. The NTSB determined the probable cause was improper maintenance that resulted in separation of the right main landing gear and loss of control during landing.
