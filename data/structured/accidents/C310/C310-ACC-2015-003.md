# Narrative Summary

The noninstrument-rated private pilot departed on a personal cross-country flight and later flew north of his intended route into rising mountainous terrain, where the airplane impacted terrain in instrument conditions. All four occupants were fatally injured and the airplane was destroyed.

Before the accident leg, the pilot showed repeated confusion during radio communications, misidentified the airplane type, inaccurately reported position information, and later departed without required coordination, causing a runway incursion. Radar data showed the airplane then turned north instead of remaining on course to the intended Texas destination.

The investigation found no record that the pilot held a multiengine rating, no record of transition training in the accident airplane, and no evidence of a current flight review. The pilot's recovered logbook showed only about 35.4 hours in the accident make and model.

The airplane had not received the required annual inspection for continued airworthiness. Postaccident examination revealed no mechanical anomalies that would have precluded normal operation.

The event was consistent with continued VFR flight into IMC and terrain by a low-time-in-type pilot with incomplete training in the aircraft and lapsed pilot currency.
