# Narrative Summary

The non-instrument-rated pilot returned to Catalina, boarded two passengers, and departed as weather conditions were deteriorating rapidly.

He reported conducting an abbreviated run-up during taxi, and the left engine failed 2 to 3 seconds after liftoff.

On-scene examination found the left fuel selector valve positioned between OFF and its normal takeoff setting, while engine testing later showed no mechanical malfunction that would have prevented normal operation.

Flow testing demonstrated that the valve, in its as-found position, could not supply the fuel flow required for takeoff power, which resulted in left-engine fuel starvation in the initial climb.

The airplane entered cloud or fog, impacted terrain shortly after departure, and a postimpact fire followed.
