# Narrative Summary

On June 10, 2016, at 1610 eastern daylight time, a Cessna 310G, N8943Z, collided with a berm while landing at Orlando Apopka Airport in Apopka, Florida. The airline transport pilot was not injured, and the airplane sustained substantial damage. The flight was conducted under Part 91 as a local post-maintenance test flight following an annual inspection, with visual meteorological conditions prevailing.

The accident flight was the airplane's first flight after the annual inspection, during which no brake-related discrepancies were noted. The pilot performed two touch-and-go landings and one go-around before attempting a full-stop landing. The touchdown was normal, and the pilot retracted the flaps during the landing roll, waiting to apply brakes until approaching the runway end.

Upon applying brakes, the left brake failed to function while the right brake worked, causing the airplane to drift right. The pilot secured the engines and applied right braking to slow the aircraft, but it exited the right side of the runway and impacted upsloping terrain, resulting in damage to the left horizontal stabilizer.

Post-accident examination revealed intermittent failure of the left brake master cylinder due to contamination and degradation. The right brake functioned normally, and no leaks were found in the system. The airplane had not been operated for the previous two years prior to the inspection.
