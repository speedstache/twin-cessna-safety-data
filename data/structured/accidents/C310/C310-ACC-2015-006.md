# Narrative Summary

After a normal touchdown and while decelerating on the runway, the left main landing gear collapsed and the airplane departed the runway. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the left wing, left horizontal stabilizer, and lower fuselage.

Examination found that the left main landing gear down-lock bellcrank had fractured and separated from its trunnion. The fracture location was consistent with additional stress that could occur if the landing gear had not been properly rigged.

The service manual required a down-lock tension check during each annual inspection. Maintenance records showed that the airplane had flown only about 16 hours since the most recent annual inspection, but the records did not indicate that the gear system check had been performed.

The pilot held a private certificate with 1,485 hours total time and 153 hours in make and model. The flight was a local Part 91 personal flight in day visual conditions.

The evidence supports a maintenance-induced landing gear rigging deficiency that resulted in the bellcrank failure and gear collapse during landing.
