# Narrative Summary

On January 9, 2011, a Cessna 310C landed short of the runway at Lancaster, California, after a nose gear malfunction.

The pilot reported the nose landing gear failed to fully retract after a practice approach and he diverted to a nearby airport.

He completed the defective nose gear procedure, but with the airplane about one-quarter mile from the runway he moved the mixtures to idle cut-off and the propellers to feather.

The airplane's descent rate then increased and it touched down about 200 feet short of the runway.

Postaccident examination found substantial damage to the forward fuselage and wings.

A later examination found that the nose gear centering roller mechanism was missing, and the pilot reported that this allowed the nose wheel assembly to jam in the wheel well.

The emergency checklist directed landing before placing the mixtures to idle cut-off.
