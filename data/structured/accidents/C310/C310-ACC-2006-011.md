On October 27, 2006, a Cessna 310N, N56MB, sustained substantial damage during landing at Lake Havasu City Airport, Arizona. The Part 91 personal flight was conducted in day VMC. The private pilot and two passengers were not injured. During the approach to runway 32, ground witnesses observed the airplane with the landing gear retracted throughout the approach. During the landing flare, the left propeller contacted the runway surface. The pilot initiated a go‑around; however, he reported that the airplane’s performance was insufficient to complete a normal traffic pattern. He maneuvered in a 180‑degree turn back toward the runway and extended the landing gear. Upon touchdown, the airplane veered off the runway into a dirt area and the left main landing gear collapsed. A postaccident examination by an FAA mechanic found no mechanical malfunctions that would have precluded normal gear operation. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s failure to extend the landing gear prior to touchdown.
