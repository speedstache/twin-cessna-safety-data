# Narrative Summary

On December 27, 2007, a Cessna 310R, N37249, was destroyed when it impacted trees and terrain during an ILS approach to runway 28 at Cherry Capital Airport, Traverse City, Michigan. The Part 91 personal flight was operating in instrument meteorological conditions at night. Radar data showed the airplane remained within the lateral bounds of the localizer but did not maintain a stabilized descent on the glideslope. The airplane remained above the upper bound of the glideslope beam for approximately two minutes prior to the end of recorded data. The final recorded descent rate was approximately 1,500 feet per minute, and the average airspeed during the last minute of flight was about 77 knots. Weather conditions included a 1,700-foot overcast ceiling and an AIRMET for moderate icing below 8,000 feet. Air traffic control had advised the pilot of reported icing conditions prior to the approach. Postaccident examination of the airframe and engines revealed no preimpact mechanical anomalies. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s failure to maintain a proper glidepath during the instrument approach, resulting in loss of control and impact with terrain.
