# Narrative Summary

On July 18, 2023, the Cessna T310R departed Santa Fe Regional Airport on a Part 91 personal flight. Shortly after takeoff, the pilot reported an engine failure and later confirmed one engine was operating. ADS-B data showed the airplane climbed to about 200 feet above ground level and then continued without further climb. The airplane entered a descending left turn and impacted a house after the left wing dropped near the end of the flight. Examination found major internal damage in the left engine and separated crankshaft counterweight components in the oil sump. The aft-facing crankshaft counterweight retaining rings had been installed incorrectly. The right engine showed the same retaining-ring installation condition, but its counterweights remained intact. The landing gear was retracted and the left propeller was feathered. The reported density altitude at Santa Fe was 8,769 feet. The NTSB determined that improper retaining-ring installation caused the left engine power loss and that inadequate airspeed following the power loss contributed to loss of control.
