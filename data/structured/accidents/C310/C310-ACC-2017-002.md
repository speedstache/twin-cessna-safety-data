# Narrative Summary

On September 1, 2017, a Cessna 310G sustained substantial fuselage damage during a landing gear collapse at Rocky Mountain Metropolitan Airport.
The flight was conducted under Part 91 as an instructional training flight in visual meteorological conditions.
During the first landing attempt at Greeley-Weld County Airport, the pilot felt a mushy touchdown even though the green gear-down indicator was illuminated.
The pilot aborted that landing and attempted a second landing with the landing gear left down.
After the second aborted landing, neither the red gear-up light nor the green gear-down light was illuminated.
The pilot diverted to Rocky Mountain Metropolitan Airport, where tower personnel confirmed that the right main landing gear was not fully extended.
Attempts to lower the landing gear using emergency procedures were unsuccessful.
The airplane landed and the right landing gear collapsed.
Maintenance personnel found a bent pushrod from the gear motor to the right main landing gear, and the reason for the bent pushrod was not determined.
