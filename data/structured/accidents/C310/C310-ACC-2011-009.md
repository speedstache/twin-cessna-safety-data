# Narrative Summary

On March 19, 2011, a Cessna T310R crashed during a missed approach at Butte, Montana.

The pilot had been advised before the approach that weather at the destination had deteriorated below instrument approach minimums.

Despite that information, he continued the approach and later reported executing the missed approach.

The accident occurred in rapidly worsening snow, fog, low visibility, and icing conditions.

The pilot had recently purchased the airplane and reported limited solo IMC experience in it.

The wreckage pattern was consistent with a high-speed, steep nose-down, left-turning descent with both engines producing power at impact.

Postaccident examination did not identify an airframe or engine failure that would have precluded normal operation.
