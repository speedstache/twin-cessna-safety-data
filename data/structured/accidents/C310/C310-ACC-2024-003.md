# Narrative Summary

On December 30, 2024, the Cessna 310D was operated on a Part 91 ferry flight from Coldwater, Michigan, to West Union, Ohio. The owner reported that the airplane did not have a current annual inspection and that a ferry permit had been issued. The owner stated that maintenance had been performed before the flight and that the pilot elected to fly with the landing gear down. Before departure, the airplane received 57.5 gallons of 100LL fuel. The fueler reported that one main tank leaked when filled above about half capacity and that the right auxiliary tank was not filled because its bladder leaked. During the flight, the pilot communicated that the airplane was operating normally and later reported being 20 to 30 miles from the destination before beginning descent. A witness near the accident site reported hearing both engines cutting out and then hearing no engine noise before impact. The airplane impacted trees and terrain on a hilltop. The report is preliminary and the wreckage was retained for further examination.
