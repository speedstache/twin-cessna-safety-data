# Narrative Summary

On December 13, 2007, a Cessna 310J, N310RV, sustained substantial damage during a go-around at Hilton Head Airport, South Carolina. The Part 91 personal flight was conducted in day VMC. During the landing flare on runway 21, the airplane ballooned and floated beyond approximately two-thirds of the 4,300-foot runway. The pilot advanced the throttles to initiate a go-around; however, the airplane rolled and yawed to the right and departed the runway surface. The pilot reported that at the time of the go-around the airplane was below Vmc, and the right engine either lost power or failed to attain full power. A pilot-rated passenger reported the airplane was “very slow,” about 80 knots, which was below the published Vmc of 87 mph and below the recommended single-engine safe speed of 102 mph. Examination of the wreckage revealed no preimpact mechanical malfunctions. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s failure to maintain aircraft control during the go-around.
