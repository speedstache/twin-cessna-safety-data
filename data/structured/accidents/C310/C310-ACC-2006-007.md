## Narrative

On April 15, 2006, at 1700 local time, a Cessna 310Q experienced a hard landing during a visual approach to runway 32 at Finleyville Airpark, Pennsylvania. The private pilot was operating under Part 91 in day visual meteorological conditions. The 2,505-foot-long runway was dry asphalt. On final approach, the airplane was configured with 15 degrees of flaps, landing gear extended, manifold pressure set to 18 inches, and an airspeed of 118 mph. Approximately 700 feet from the runway threshold, the pilot extended the flaps to 35 degrees and reduced airspeed to 100 mph. The airplane began to sink, and although full power was applied, it touched down hard in a dirt field about 100 feet short of the runway threshold. The airplane came to rest upright off the right side of the runway and sustained substantial damage. The pilot reported no pre-impact mechanical malfunctions. Reported winds were from 320 degrees at 13 knots, gusting to 18 knots.
