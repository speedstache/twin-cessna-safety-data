# Narrative Summary

The pilot was conducting a Part 91 personal flight and was on a special VFR approach to Nome, Alaska.

He reported that the airplane was about 4 miles from the airport when it collided with terrain during a turn.

The report stated that there were no mechanical problems with the airplane before impact.

Weather observations near the accident site showed 2 miles visibility with scattered clouds at 800 ft agl and a broken ceiling at 1,500 ft agl.

The airplane sustained substantial damage to the wings and fuselage, and the pilot received minor injuries.
