# Narrative Summary

On November 9, 2024, the Cessna 310K landed at McGhee Tyson Airport in Alcoa, Tennessee, on a Part 91 business flight. The pilot reported that the approach and landing were normal. After exiting the runway onto a taxiway, the airplane swerved right and appeared to accelerate. The pilot was unable to control braking and steering, and the airplane departed the taxiway. The airplane crossed a steep depression and collided with a wind sensor pole. The right wing and fuselage sustained substantial damage. Postaccident examination found that the brass splined bushing on the left engine throttle control shaft was stripped. The failure produced an uncontrollable full-power condition on the left engine. The wheel brakes were examined and no anomalies were found.
