# Narrative Summary

On February 21, 2011, a Cessna 310Q sustained substantial damage during a hard landing at Romeoville, Illinois.

The pilot was descending on an IFR flight when the airplane began accumulating ice near the destination.

She cycled the deice boots several times but reported that she could not determine how much ice had accumulated on the airframe.

The approach was flown using normal approach speeds rather than speeds adjusted for icing conditions.

The airplane touched down hard, and the internal structure of the left wing was substantially damaged.

Ice was visible along the leading edge of the wings after landing.

The pilot reported no other mechanical malfunction that would have precluded normal operation.
