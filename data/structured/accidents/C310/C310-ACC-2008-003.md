# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed on a cross-country flight over mountainous terrain in day visual conditions and filed a VFR flight plan that was not activated. An alert notification was issued when the airplane did not arrive at its destination. The airplane struck trees in a shallow descent into slightly rising terrain at an elevation of about 9,800 feet msl and was destroyed. Examination of the airplane and both engines revealed no mechanical discrepancies. Postaccident toxicology testing was consistent with impairment due to marijuana use within three hours of the accident. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s failure to maintain clearance from mountainous terrain, with pilot impairment due to marijuana as a contributing factor.
