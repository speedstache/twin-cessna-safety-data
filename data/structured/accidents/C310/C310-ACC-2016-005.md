# Narrative Summary

The airline transport pilot departed Columbia, California, in a Cessna 310B on a personal cross-country flight with three passengers. Shortly after takeoff, witnesses saw the airplane drift left, stop climbing, descend, and collide with terrain off the left side of the runway. The airplane was consumed by a postcrash fire, and all four occupants were fatally injured.

Video analysis estimated the airplane's ground speed during the last 5 seconds of flight at about 71 knots. Both engines showed evidence of power at impact, and postaccident examination of the airframe and engines did not identify mechanical anomalies that would have precluded normal operation. The investigation found undocumented fuel-injection-related engine modifications and could not locate records or revised performance data associated with those alterations.

The reported temperature was 40 degrees C at an airport elevation of 2,121 ft, producing a calculated density altitude of 5,483 ft. The pilot had about 12,000 hours total time but only about 1 hour in the accident make and model. The NTSB determined that the pilot failed to maintain control during takeoff initial climb for reasons that could not be determined.
