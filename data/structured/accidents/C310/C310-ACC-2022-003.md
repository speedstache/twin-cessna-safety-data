# Narrative Summary

The accident flight was the first flight after the airplane had been painted and flight controls had been removed. Immediately after takeoff, the pilot needed significant left rudder to maintain directional control. He performed a precautionary landing on airport property, landing hard in the grass at an angle to the runway. The nose landing gear fractured, and the fuselage sustained substantial damage. Postaccident examination found the rudder trim system significantly out of rig, and maintenance records for the rudder removal and reinstallation were not provided. The pilot stated that he may not have checked the control trims that were part of the preflight checklist.
