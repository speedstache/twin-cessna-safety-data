# Narrative Summary

On March 24, 2019, a Cessna T310R was being used for aerial survey training for a newly hired pilot.
The flight instructor reported that the airplane was slowed to mission airspeed with reduced power and more than 15° of flaps.
The instructor pulled the landing gear warning circuit breaker to silence the warning horn for communication clarity.
The pilot receiving instruction reported that he did not know the instructor had pulled the landing gear warning circuit breaker.
While returning to Northeast Philadelphia Airport, the landing checklist was reported as accomplished 15 to 20 miles from the airport.
The instructor saw that the pilot was falling behind on power and descent management and began assisting as the airplane was high and fast.
The instructor selected the landing gear down and added full flaps, but neither pilot noticed the absence of a green down-and-locked indication.
The airplane landed with the landing gear retracted and sustained substantial damage to the fuselage and both engines.
Postaccident gear extension tests found no defects, and the NTSB found it likely that the landing gear motor circuit breaker had been inadvertently pulled.
