# Narrative Summary

On March 30, 2011, a Cessna 310R struck trees and terrain while conducting the RNAV GPS runway 09 approach to Pikeville, Kentucky.

The Part 135 on-demand passenger flight was operating in instrument meteorological conditions to a mountaintop airport obscured by clouds and fog.

Radar data showed the airplane aligned with the runway but descending below the published minimum descent altitude.

Witnesses below airport elevation saw the airplane emerge from beneath the clouds on top of a ridgeline and then hit treetops.

An airport employee had advised the pilot that the weather was worse than the automated report indicated.

Postaccident examination found no preimpact mechanical anomalies.

Toxicology identified nighttime cold medication at levels above the therapeutic range.
