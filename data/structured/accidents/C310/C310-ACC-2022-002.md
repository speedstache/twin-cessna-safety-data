# Narrative Summary

The pilot back-taxied for departure and began a 180-degree left turn to align with the runway centerline. During the turn, the right main landing gear collapsed and the wing, propeller, and right elevator contacted the runway. Postaccident examination found fractures of right main landing gear components with features consistent with overstress. The airplane had recently been repaired following another right main landing gear failure. The pilot, who was also the mechanic, reported adjusting the downlock tension within maintenance manual limits before returning the airplane to service. The NTSB determined that the right main landing gear components failed for undetermined reasons.
