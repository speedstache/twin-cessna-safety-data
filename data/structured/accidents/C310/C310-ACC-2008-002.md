# Narrative Summary

The pilot conducted a Part 91 personal round-trip flight and relied on fuel gauge indications for the auxiliary tanks rather than visually checking them. Before the return flight, the pilot did not look into any fuel tanks and estimated the airplane had about 30 gallons on board. On final approach to Compton Airport in day VMC, the left engine began sputtering and the pilot changed fuel selector positions multiple times, including crossfeed. Moments later both engines lost power on short final, and the airplane impacted residences about 2,100 feet short of the runway threshold. Postaccident examination found no preimpact mechanical malfunction of the fuel system or engines and only small amounts of fuel in the fuel lines, with no fuel in the tanks. The NTSB determined the loss of both engines’ power was due to fuel exhaustion resulting from inadequate preflight inspection and failure to verify fuel quantity.
