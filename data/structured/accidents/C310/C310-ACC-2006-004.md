# Narrative Summary

Following a preflight inspection and taxi, the pilot initiated takeoff on a 4,000-foot runway. Witnesses reported that the airplane became airborne before the runway midpoint in a tail-low pitch attitude. Shortly after liftoff, the airplane banked and drifted left and right while exhibiting lateral wobbling and a mush or stall condition.

The aircraft impacted trees approximately 450 feet beyond the departure end of the runway in a left bank. Examination of the wreckage revealed no evidence of preimpact mechanical failure. The National Transportation Safety Board determined that the probable cause was the pilot’s failure to maintain sufficient airspeed during takeoff-initial climb, resulting in an aerodynamic stall/mush. There were notes in the report of water being found in the left gascolator, but there was none found in the tank. This was not noted as a cause in the report. 

Likeliest cause of crash is departure stall caused by inability to maintain airspeed.
