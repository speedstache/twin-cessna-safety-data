# Narrative Summary

The pilot was relocating the airplane after maintenance that included a leak check of the left-wing tip fuel tank. About 2.5 gallons of fuel had been added for the leak check, and fueling records indicated the pilot did not refuel before departure. During the approach to the destination airport, witnesses heard popping sounds and observed the airplane flying slowly with the landing gear and flaps extended. The left propeller showed minimal rotational signatures, and visual evidence indicated it was not feathered at impact. Examination found no preaccident mechanical malfunctions or anomalies that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB determined that the left engine lost power due to fuel exhaustion and that the pilot did not follow the one-engine-inoperative checklist or maintain minimum controllable airspeed.
