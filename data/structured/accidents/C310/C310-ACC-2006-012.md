On September 14, 2006, a Cessna 310P, N5858M, was substantially damaged during landing on runway 02 at Kennett Memorial Airport, Missouri. The Part 91 personal flight was conducted in day VMC. The airline transport pilot and two pilot-rated passengers were not injured. The pilot reported a normal approach and confirmed three green gear indications prior to landing. Within seconds of touchdown, the airplane veered left of the runway centerline. The airplane departed the runway surface and the left main landing gear collapsed during the rollout. Examination revealed that the left main landing gear torque links had separated at the center hinge point, allowing the wheel assembly to rotate 180 degrees. The washers installed at the pivot hinge were undersized compared to those specified in the manufacturer’s service bulletin, which required larger-diameter replacement washers. Maintenance records did not show documented compliance with the service bulletin. The NTSB determined the probable cause was separation of the left main landing gear torque link during landing, with contributing factors including failure to comply with the manufacturer’s service bulletin and loss of directional control following the separation.
