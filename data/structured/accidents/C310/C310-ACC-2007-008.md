# Narrative Summary

On December 18, 2007, a Cessna 310R, N5499J, sustained substantial damage during taxi at Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport, Arizona, following a Part 135 air taxi cargo flight. After landing on runway 25L at night, the pilot exited onto taxiway Hotel and proceeded toward the south cargo apron. Rather than following the painted taxiway lead-in lines, the pilot cut diagonally across the non-movement apron area toward the parking location. While taxiing at an estimated 15 to 20 miles per hour, the pilot failed to observe a line of container carts parked on the apron. The airplane skidded approximately 50 feet before colliding with one of the carts. The left main landing gear sheared off and the left wing sustained substantial structural damage. Postaccident interviews and examination revealed no mechanical malfunctions. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s selection of an improper taxi route, inadequate visual lookout, failure to maintain separation from ground obstacles, and excessive taxi speed.
