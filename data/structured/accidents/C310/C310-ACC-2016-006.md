# Narrative Summary

The airline transport pilot departed Wickenburg, Arizona, in a Cessna 310N with three passengers on a Part 91 personal flight to Payson. Shortly after rotation, the airplane veered right, failed to climb, rolled sharply right, and struck a refuse transfer trailer about 2,200 ft beyond the departure threshold. The airplane sustained substantial damage and all four occupants were seriously injured.

Examination showed that the right engine lost power because a clear gelatinous contaminant blocked the inlet port of the fuel flow transducer, resulting in fuel starvation. Laboratory analysis identified the material as silicone. Smaller fragments were found farther downstream in the right-engine fuel system, but investigators could not determine the source of the contamination.

The departure occurred from a runway with an uphill gradient and rising terrain beyond the departure end. Temperature and field elevation produced a density altitude of about 4,200 ft, and the pilot reported a takeoff weight of 4,900 pounds. The pilot had about 18,000 hours total time, about 1,000 hours in make and model, and his last flight review was reported as March 2014.
