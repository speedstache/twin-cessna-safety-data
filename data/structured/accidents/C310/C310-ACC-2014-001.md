# Narrative Summary

During cruise flight, both engines experienced a partial loss of power, and the pilot conducted a forced landing in an open field. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the wings and fuselage during the landing.

The pilot reported that the engines lost power because the airplane ran out of fuel. He stated that he had requested that the main tanks be filled before departure, but he did not verify that the fueling had actually occurred.

The flight was conducted under Part 91 as a personal flight in day visual meteorological conditions. No preaccident mechanical malfunctions or failures were reported.

The pilot had 3,528 hours total time and 80 hours in make and model. He had flown 83 hours in the previous 90 days.

The accident sequence was consistent with fuel exhaustion following an inadequate preflight fuel check and an assumption that requested fueling had been completed.
