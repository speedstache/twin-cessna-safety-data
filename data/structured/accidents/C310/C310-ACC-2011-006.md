# Narrative Summary

On April 10, 2011, a Cessna 310R collided with trees during approach to McComb, Mississippi.

The pilot and two passengers had arrived at the departure airport after going to a bar.

No weather briefing was obtained and no IFR flight plan was filed, although night instrument meteorological conditions existed at the destination.

The pilot later told air traffic control that the destination airport was in sight and canceled flight following while still 24 miles away and above cloud layers.

The airplane overflew the airport, turned left, and the last radar target was recorded at 2,600 feet mean sea level.

Wreckage examination and onboard engine analyzer data did not reveal any preimpact mechanical malfunction.

Toxicology showed alcohol in blood, urine, and vitreous samples.
