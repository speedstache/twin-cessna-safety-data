# Narrative Summary

Shortly before departure, the pilot received a weather briefing that warned of thunderstorms near the destination and recommended obtaining an update before takeoff. The pilot did not call back for the update and departed on the short IFR cross-country flight.

During the approach, controllers advised the pilot of precipitation and thunderstorms in the vicinity of the destination airport. After the pilot initiated a missed approach because the airport was not in sight, radar showed the airplane complete a climbing right turn in extreme precipitation and then enter a rapid descent into the river.

Postaccident examination found no preimpact mechanical malfunctions that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB concluded that the airplane likely encountered thunderstorm-related turbulence or windshear during the missed approach.

The pilot held a commercial certificate and had about 1,000 hours total time, about 407 hours multiengine time, and about 300 hours in the accident make and model based on insurance documentation. Instrument recency was not documented in the report.

The event was consistent with continued flight into known convective weather conditions, followed by loss of control during a missed approach.
