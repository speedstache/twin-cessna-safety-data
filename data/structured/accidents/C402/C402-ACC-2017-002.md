# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed Tampa International Airport on the third leg of a four-leg Part 135 non-scheduled passenger trip.
About 13 minutes after departure, he advised air traffic control that the airplane was fuel critical and requested vectors to the nearest airport.
Both engines subsequently lost total power.
The pilot performed a forced landing on a street about two miles from Albert Whitted Airport, and the airplane collided with two vehicles.
Both main wingtip fuel tanks were separated from the wings, and both auxiliary fuel tanks were breached.
The fuel selectors were found positioned to their respective main tanks.
Fuel consumption calculations indicated that the airplane would have used about the capacity of the main tanks since its most recent refueling.
The pilot had 38 hours in make and model, completed his Part 135 checkride the day before, and the accident trip was his first solo revenue flight.
