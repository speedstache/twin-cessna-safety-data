# Narrative Summary

Two pilots were conducting new-hire training in a Cessna 402B near Sioux Falls, South Dakota.
While maneuvering for a practice approach, the flight crew noticed that the left engine was running rough.
The left-seat pilot activated the auxiliary fuel pump, and the roughness temporarily resolved.
Shortly afterward, the left engine tachometer went to zero, and flames were observed from the left engine cowling.
The crew declared an emergency, secured the left engine, and landed the airplane without further incident.
Postaccident examination found substantial thermal damage to the left wing structure and firewall.
The No. 4 cylinder had burned through at the exhaust port.
The cylinder examination was consistent with a preexisting crack condition that allowed exhaust gas erosion and led to the engine fire.
