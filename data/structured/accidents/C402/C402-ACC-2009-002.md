# Narrative Summary

The Part 135 cargo flight encountered moderate icing during the initial climb and continued in instrument meteorological conditions.
The pilot used deice boots, pitot heat, stall vane heat, propeller deice, and windshield hot plate, and later climbed above the cloud tops.
Ice remained on unprotected wing and windshield areas when the pilot began descent and was vectored for an instrument approach.
While maneuvering to join the ILS approach, additional ice accumulated on unprotected surfaces and the windshield became largely obscured except for the hot plate area.
The airplane crossed the runway threshold at high indicated airspeed and the pilot sensed a high sink rate immediately prior to touchdown.
A hard landing resulted in substantial right wing structural damage, and postflight inspection found 1 to 1.5 inches of ice on unprotected areas with strong gusting crosswinds reported.
