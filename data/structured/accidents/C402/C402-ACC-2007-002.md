# Narrative Summary

A Cessna 402C was conducting a Part 135 on-demand cargo flight to Cordova, Alaska. During the
landing roll on a hard-surfaced runway, the airplane veered right and the pilot was unable to
maintain directional control. The right main landing gear collapsed, resulting in substantial damage
to the right wing spar. Inspection found the right main landing gear scissors link became
disconnected after the securing bolt and washer pulled through the scissor bushing opening. Cessna
Service Information Letter ME83-37 recommended replacing the original outer scissors link washers
with larger washers to improve service life and alignment retention. The operator had not
incorporated the non-mandatory service letter and the airplane remained equipped with the smaller
washers. The NTSB determined the right main gear linkage failed during the landing roll, and noted
maintenance personnel did not comply with the manufacturer service letter.
