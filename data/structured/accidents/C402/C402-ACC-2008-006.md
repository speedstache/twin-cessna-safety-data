# Narrative Summary

During a Part 91 instructional flight, the crew landed at Leesburg International Airport in a Cessna
402C. During the landing roll, the left main landing gear separated from the airplane and the left
wing contacted the runway. The airplane exited the runway and came to rest in grass, sustaining
substantial damage. Postaccident examination found the left main landing gear cylinder lower end had
corrosion, pitting, and fatigue cracking. Maintenance records indicated a prior visual inspection of
the landing gear cylinder in accordance with manufacturer guidance. The NTSB determined the gear
separation resulted from fatigue cracking of the cylinder, and cited an inadequate inspection
procedure as a factor.
