# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed Nantucket Memorial Airport on a personal cross-country flight.
After liftoff and acceleration, he encountered high nose-down control forces and increasing difficulty maintaining control.
He used manual trim and verified that the autopilot was not engaged, but the nose-down tendency continued.
The pilot rejected the takeoff and made an emergency landing on the remaining runway.
Postaccident examination found the elevator trim pushrod separated from the elevator trim tab actuator and wedged against the elevator main spar.
The elevator trim tab was found deflected well beyond its normal nose-down limit.
The airplane had recently undergone an annual inspection and painting, and the right elevator had been removed during that work.
The required replacement hardware specified by the applicable elevator trim pushrod AD had not been installed before the airplane was returned to service.
