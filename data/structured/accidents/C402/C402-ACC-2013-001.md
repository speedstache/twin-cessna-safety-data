# Narrative Summary

Before the accident flight, maintenance had been conducted on the airplane at an FAA-authorized repair station.
After takeoff, the pilot selected the landing gear up and observed that the main gear retracted but the nose gear did not.
The pilot completed the emergency checklist, selected the gear down, and observed three gear down-and-locked indications.
The pilot returned to Fort Lauderdale Executive Airport and made a normal touchdown.
During the landing roll, the nose landing gear collapsed and the nose and propellers contacted the runway.
Examination found the nose landing gear actuator intact and extended, but the actuator rod end was not connected to the nose landing gear drag brace.
Maintenance records did not show work on the nose landing gear assembly, but other documents indicated nondestructive testing had been requested on the nose landing gear drag brace.
A mechanic later reported that he had disconnected and removed the actuator bolt at the request of the NDT technician, and the bolt was not reinstalled before return to service.
