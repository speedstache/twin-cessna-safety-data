# Narrative Summary

A Cessna 402B departed on a Part 91 personal flight and later entered a holding pattern due to
weather at the destination. The pilot executed multiple instrument approaches before being vectored
for another approach. During the subsequent maneuvering, both engines lost power due to fuel
exhaustion. The airplane impacted terrain and was substantially damaged; the pilot and passengers
sustained minor injuries. The NTSB determined the pilot’s improper in-flight fuel management and
inadequate preflight planning resulted in fuel exhaustion. Weather-related delays and repeated
approach attempts were part of the operational context preceding the power loss.
