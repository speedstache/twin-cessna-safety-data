# Narrative Summary

During climb out from Boston, Massachusetts, the pilot noticed both left and right hydraulic flow lights flicker. The pilot attempted to extend the landing gear before all hydraulic pressure was lost, but only the left main landing gear green light illuminated. The pilot returned to the departure airport and performed the emergency landing gear extension checklist. The right and nose landing gear did not extend, and the airplane landed with only the left main landing gear extended. The airplane sustained substantial damage, and the pilot and two passengers were not injured. Postaccident examination found a fractured solid hydraulic line connected to the hydraulic filter and no hydraulic fluid in the system. Maintenance records did not determine the age of the hydraulic line or whether maintenance had been performed on it. Postaccident testing showed that the emergency landing gear extension system functioned normally when the activation handle was fully extended.
