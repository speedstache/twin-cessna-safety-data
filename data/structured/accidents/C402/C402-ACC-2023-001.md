# Narrative Summary

The pilot was completing an on-demand Part 135 flight to Fort Lauderdale, Florida. He reported that the airplane touched down on the wet 8,000-foot runway about midfield. The pilot stated that the left brake did not work and that he avoided applying too much right brake to prevent directional-control loss. The airplane traveled off the end of the runway into an engineered materials arresting system and came to rest upright. A postaccident examination occurred two days later after the pilot had begun repairs without authorization. The left brake assembly had been removed and was not available for examination. The left main landing gear tire had a large flat spot, which suggested the brake was functioning and the wheel was likely skidding on the wet runway. The NTSB determined that ineffective brake use resulted in the runway overrun.
