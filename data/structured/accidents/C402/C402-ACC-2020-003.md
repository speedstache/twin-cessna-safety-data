# Narrative Summary

The commercial pilot and check pilot were conducting their first long-duration aerial observation flight in a recently acquired Cessna 402C. They departed with full fuel, completed about five hours of aerial observation, and began returning to the destination airport. About 15 miles from the airport, the left engine fuel warning light illuminated, and within a few seconds the right engine stopped producing power. The crew attempted a restart and diverted toward a closer airport. Both engines later lost total power about three miles from the alternate airport. The crew elected to land on a highway, but power briefly returned to the left engine during the landing flare. The airplane climbed and rolled, then stalled and impacted trees and terrain. The flight instructor reported that there was no fuel in the airplane after the accident, and the FAA found no evidence of fuel spillage from the breached tanks.
