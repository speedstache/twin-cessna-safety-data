# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed St. Lucie County International Airport for a short personal flight to Witham Field Airport.
Before departure, he checked the fuel gauges and calculated that the airplane had approximately one hour of fuel on board.
He did not report visually checking the fuel quantity in the tanks before the accident flight.
While on final approach, the left engine lost power, and the pilot repositioned the left fuel selector to the left inboard tank.
The left engine restarted, but the right engine then lost power, and the left engine subsequently quit again.
The pilot made a forced landing in a field where the airplane struck a tree and terrain.
Postaccident examination found the left wing tanks empty and no evidence of fuel in the right wing tanks.
The engines and airplane systems showed no anomalies that would have precluded normal operation.
