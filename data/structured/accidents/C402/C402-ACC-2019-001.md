# Narrative Summary

The pilot was conducting a scheduled Part 135 cargo flight to Montague Airport.
On final approach, he selected full flaps and slowed the airplane to 85 to 90 knots.
After touchdown, he applied the brakes and observed minor braking action from the left brake and no braking action from the right brake.
The pilot retracted the flaps and applied full power for a go-around but determined that there was insufficient runway and airspeed remaining to regain flight.
He reduced power to idle and applied heavy braking while attempting to maintain directional control.
The airplane departed the runway end off the left side and came to rest in a dirt overrun area.
The nosewheel collapsed during the excursion, and the forward fuselage was substantially damaged.
The runway was wet and likely icy due to rain the night before and subfreezing overnight temperatures.
