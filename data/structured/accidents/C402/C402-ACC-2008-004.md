# Narrative Summary

A commercial pilot departed on a Part 91 personal repositioning flight in a Cessna 402B after adding
10 gallons of fuel to each main tank. Approximately five minutes after takeoff at about 1,000 ft,
the airplane experienced a partial loss of engine power. The pilot attempted to manage the power
loss and the airplane subsequently impacted terrain and sustained substantial damage. The pilot
received serious injuries. The NTSB determined the partial loss of engine power was due to fuel
starvation and noted the pilot’s decision to add only a limited amount of fuel prior to the flight.
The NTSB also cited inaccurate fuel selector valve placards depicting the fuel tank configuration as
a contributing factor.
