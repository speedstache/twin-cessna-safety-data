# Narrative Summary

A Cessna 402C operating a Part 135 cargo flight experienced abnormal fuel quantity indications in
flight, with the right fuel gauge decreasing while the left remained constant. The pilot attempted
to crossfeed from the left tank and set the auxiliary pumps to LOW. The right engine subsequently
lost power and was secured, followed by loss of power to the remaining engine during the crossfeed
attempt. The airplane made a forced landing and sustained substantial damage. The NTSB determined
both engines lost power due to a fitting failure and fuel leak at the right fuel selector valve that
allowed fuel from the right tank to be lost overboard, and then introduced air into the left engine
fuel lines during the attempted crossfeed operation.
