# Narrative Summary

A Cessna 402 was conducting a Part 91 positioning flight in night instrument meteorological
conditions. After takeoff, the airplane made a slight left turn followed by a right turn that
continued until radar contact was lost. The airplane reached a maximum altitude of about 700 feet
before impacting terrain approximately 3 miles northwest of the departure airport. The airplane was
destroyed and the pilot was fatally injured. The NTSB determined the accident resulted from a loss
of aircraft control due to spatial disorientation.
