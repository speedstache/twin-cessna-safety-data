# Narrative Summary

A Cessna 402 operating a Part 135 sightseeing flight departed Henderson, Nevada, and the pilot
reported excessive drag after landing gear retraction. The pilot conducted a low pass and witnesses
observed the right main landing gear appeared twisted. The pilot proceeded to the intended
destination where emergency services were present. The airplane executed a gear-up landing in gravel
near the runway and sustained substantial damage. The NTSB determined the left main landing gear
torque link assembly failed for undetermined reasons.
