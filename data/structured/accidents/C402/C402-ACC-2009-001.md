# Narrative Summary

The Part 135 scheduled passenger flight departed Key West, Florida, and later experienced a total loss of engine power during cruise.
Earlier in the day the pilot noted increasing fuel quantity imbalance indications and attempted crossfeed to balance the tanks.
During the incident flight the pilot repositioned the left fuel selector for crossfeed and then attempted to return it to the main tank position but did not achieve the detent.
Both engines subsequently drew fuel from the right main tank until fuel starvation occurred, resulting in engine surging and loss of power.
The pilot declared an emergency, feathered both propellers, used the emergency blowdown bottle for gear extension, and landed uneventfully at Naples Municipal Airport.
Postincident testing found the left fuel selector would not reliably travel into the detent due to inadequate lubrication, and the operator had misinterpreted service recommendations and used an incorrect lubricant.
