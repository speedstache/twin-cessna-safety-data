# Narrative Summary

During approach on a Part 135 flight, the pilot observed down-and-locked indications for only the
nose and left main landing gear. Attempts to obtain a right main down-and-locked indication were
unsuccessful. An air traffic controller performed a visual inspection and reported the landing gear
appeared down and locked. During landing, the right main landing gear collapsed and the airplane
sustained substantial damage. The NTSB determined the right main gear collapsed due to improper
adjustment of the right main landing gear downlock by maintenance personnel.
