# Narrative Summary

The Part 135 cargo flight departed from Moab, Utah, with light snow falling and reported snow on the runway.
During the takeoff roll the airplane encountered deeper snow and began to veer left of the centerline.
The pilot attempted to correct and then rejected the takeoff.
The airplane exited the runway and came to rest beside the runway with the nose gear collapsed, sustaining substantial damage.
Witnesses and the airport manager reported heavy snowfall and runway snow depth of 4 to 5 inches at the time of the accident.
The airport manager was in the process of closing the airport for snow removal but the runway had not been closed before the attempted departure.
