# Narrative Summary

During approach to Lea County Regional Airport, the pilot selected landing gear down and the left
main down indicator did not illuminate. The pilot executed a go-around, cycled the gear again, and
received the same indication. Controllers advised the pilot the gear appeared down after a visual
check. The pilot elected to land with the unsafe gear indication, and the left main landing gear
collapsed during the landing roll. The airplane departed the runway and struck an airport sign,
sustaining substantial damage. Examination found the left main landing gear downlock was broken and
fracture surfaces were consistent with an overload event. The NTSB determined the left main landing
gear collapsed due to overload failure of the downlock.
