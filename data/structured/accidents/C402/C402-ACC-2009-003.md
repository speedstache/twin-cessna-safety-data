# Narrative Summary

During a Part 135 scheduled passenger flight, the pilot lowered the landing gear on final and the left main gear did not indicate down and locked.
The pilot recycled the gear, completed the 'Gear Will Not Extend' checklist, activated the blowdown bottle, and coordinated with the tower before landing.
The left main landing gear collapsed during the landing rollout, resulting in minor aircraft damage and an evacuation.
A subsequent landing gear retraction test duplicated the failure and troubleshooting localized the problem to the up lock actuator.
Inspection found a small ball bearing positioned incorrectly relative to a larger bearing, and disassembly confirmed an improperly sized bearing that could block the port opening.
The investigation could not determine who replaced the bearing or when it was installed.
