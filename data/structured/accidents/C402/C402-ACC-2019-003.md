# Narrative Summary

The airplane was operated under Part 91 during a Part 135 instrument proficiency check and check airman observation flight.
After completing required maneuvers, the crew returned to Boise Air Terminal/Gowen Field.
During the landing approach, the pilot selected the landing gear down and received three green indications.
All three occupants verbally confirmed that the landing gear was down and locked.
The landing itself was uneventful.
As the airplane decelerated and began a left turn to exit the runway, the right main landing gear collapsed.
The right wing struck the ground and sustained substantial damage.
Postaccident examination found that the right main landing gear bell crank assembly pivot bolt had sheared with features consistent with ductile overstress fracture from shear loading.
