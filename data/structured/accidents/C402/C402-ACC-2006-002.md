# Narrative Summary

A commercial pilot departed South Bimini on a Part 91 VFR positioning flight to Fort Lauderdale
Executive Airport. Special weather observations at the destination reported thunderstorms, heavy
rain, and reduced visibility. During the landing roll in a thunderstorm, the airplane veered off the
runway and struck a runway sign. The collision resulted in collapse of the nose landing gear and the
propellers contacted the ground. An FAA inspector found structural damage to the fuselage following
the event. The NTSB determined the pilot continued flight into adverse weather and did not maintain
directional control during the landing roll.
