# Narrative Summary

After landing at night at Milwaukee Mitchell International Airport, a Cessna 402B and a Beech BE99
were taxiing to the cargo ramp on intersecting taxi routes. Neither pilot reported seeing the other
aircraft approaching the same taxiway intersection. The Beech 99’s right propeller struck the Cessna
402’s left wing tip fuel tank at the taxiway A/A2 intersection. The impact caused a fuel-fed fire
and both pilots evacuated their aircraft. Controllers had not issued hold-short instructions or
traffic advisories to either pilot for the intersecting movement. ASDE-X replay data showed both
aircraft taxiing at approximately 20 knots approaching the intersection. The NTSB determined both
pilots failed to adequately scan for and avoid other aircraft during taxi, and ATC did not issue a
traffic advisory.
