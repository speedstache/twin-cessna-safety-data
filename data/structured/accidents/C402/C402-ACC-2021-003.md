# Narrative Summary

The pilot was operating a scheduled Part 135 passenger flight to Provincetown, Massachusetts, in instrument meteorological conditions with rain. The operator prohibited approaches to runways shorter than 4,000 feet when the tailwind component was 5 knots or more. The destination runway was 3,502 feet long, and the wind and tailwind component increased during the approach. The NTSB performance study found that the approach became unstabilized when the airplane exceeded a sink rate of 1,000 feet per minute at 400 feet msl. The operator's General Operations Manual required an immediate go-around when the approach was not stabilized. Video showed the airplane landed normally about 500 feet from the threshold, rolled down the wet runway, then became airborne again near the runway end. The airplane entered a shallow climb, collided with trees, and caught fire. The NTSB determined that the pilot delayed the aborted-landing decision until late in the landing roll with insufficient runway remaining.
