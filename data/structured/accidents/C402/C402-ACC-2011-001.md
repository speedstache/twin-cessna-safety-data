# Narrative Summary

Shortly after takeoff on a Part 135 non-scheduled flight, the hydraulic pressure caution light illuminated and the nose gear failed to retract.
The pilot completed emergency procedures and remained with unsafe gear indications.
After an approved tower flyover to verify gear position, the pilot performed a precautionary landing.
During landing, the right main landing gear collapsed, resulting in substantial aircraft damage.
Subsequent examination and functional testing of the hydraulic and landing gear system revealed no anomalies and normal operation.
The report determined the landing gear system failed for undetermined reasons.
