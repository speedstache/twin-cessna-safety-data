# Narrative Summary

Shortly after takeoff from Kirksville, Missouri, the pilot retracted the landing gear and the airplane experienced a substantial loss of engine power. The pilot retarded the throttles to idle and landed on the remaining runway without extending the landing gear. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the rear spar caps, and the pilot was not injured. Both propellers were found in a feathered position, although the pilot reported that he did not feather them. Postaccident examination found small amounts of water in limited fuel sump locations, but the NTSB reported that this was unlikely to have affected engine performance. Both engines later ran to full power on the airplane's systems and onboard fuel, and no anomalies were found. The reason for the partial loss of engine power could not be determined.
