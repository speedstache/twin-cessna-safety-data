# Narrative Summary

About 20 minutes into the Part 135 flight, the pilot observed a low-voltage annunciation followed by a total electrical failure.
The pilot attempted troubleshooting and emergency checklist procedures, but electrical power was not restored.
Because the landing gear was hydraulically actuated by an electrically powered system, the landing gear would not extend normally.
The pilot attempted to use the emergency gear-extension T-handle, but the handle broke off from its housing and separated from the cable.
After additional troubleshooting and low passes to confirm gear position, the pilot elected to conduct a gear-up landing at Fort Lauderdale/Hollywood International Airport.
The airplane landed with the landing gear retracted and sustained substantial fuselage damage.
A postaccident examination identified electrical shorts in the left starter motor and starter relay/solenoid.
The starter relay remained closed, which likely created a high electrical load and drained the battery.
