# Narrative Summary

During a scheduled Part 135 passenger flight in instrument meteorological conditions, the Cessna
402C was cruising at 5,000 ft with the autopilot engaged when it encountered a brief severe
turbulence event. Several passengers struck the cabin ceiling; one passenger was seriously injured
and two sustained minor injuries. The pilot requested altitude and routing changes and then avoided
clouds for the remainder of the flight. The flight landed uneventfully and medical assistance was
requested for injured passengers. A passenger reported hearing precipitation-like impacts and later
observing a radar display that had previously been blank. Weather radar imagery indicated the
airplane was near radar returns ranging from VIP Level 1 to Level 4 at the time of the occurrence.
The NTSB determined the pilot did not use onboard weather radar for avoidance and continued into
known adverse weather.
