# Narrative Summary

During a Part 135 scheduled passenger flight, the pilot applied brakes during the landing roll and the airplane dipped to the right.
The lower portion of the right main landing gear separated and the airplane came to rest upright.
Postincident inspection found a fatigue crack in the right upper barrel and trunnion assembly that originated on the outer surface and propagated through most of the wall thickness.
The operator had been performing recurring visual inspections focused on leaks and internal cracking, with the last inspection completed about 62 hours before the incident.
At the time of the incident, manufacturer supplemental inspections did not specifically address detecting cracks in the affected barrel area and no required NDT inspection was in place.
The report notes prior similar events involving undetected fatigue cracking originating on the outer surface of the barrel.
