# Narrative Summary

A student pilot who had recently purchased the Cessna 340 flew with a private pilot who offered to provide instruction in the airplane. The private pilot did not hold a flight instructor certificate and did not hold a multi-engine airplane rating. The airplane had been flown from New Mexico to Georgia the previous day even though it was overdue for its annual inspection and no ferry permit had been obtained. On the accident day, the airplane flew multiple legs in Georgia before crashing during approach to Covington Municipal Airport. Witnesses and surveillance video showed a sharp right turn followed by a spiraling descent. The airplane impacted terrain and unoccupied semi-trailers and was destroyed by impact forces and postimpact fire. Postaccident examination found no airframe, engine, or propeller anomaly that would have precluded normal performance. The NTSB found that the private pilot lost control and that insufficient qualifications and poor decision making contributed to the accident.
