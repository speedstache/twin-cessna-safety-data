# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed East Texas Regional Airport to perform a functional test of a newly upgraded autopilot system. Before departure, a mechanic advised the pilot not to engage the autopilot below at least 5,000 ft above ground level. ADS-B data showed the airplane climbed east of the airport to about 2,750 ft msl. Radio and radar communications were lost about 6 minutes after takeoff, and no distress call was received. The airplane impacted wooded terrain in a nose-down, near-vertical attitude and was destroyed by impact and postimpact fire. Flight control continuity was confirmed, and both engines and propellers showed no preaccident failures that would have precluded normal operation. The autopilot system could not be examined due to impact and fire damage. The NTSB determined that the pilot lost control for undetermined reasons.
