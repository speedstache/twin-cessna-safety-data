# Narrative Summary

The pilot conducted a local Part 91 post-maintenance test flight from Fort Lauderdale Executive Airport. During the approach, the pilot observed a cockpit indication that the landing gear was down and locked. During the landing roll, the left main landing gear collapsed. The left propeller struck the runway and the airplane veered off the left side of the runway before stopping in the grass. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the left wing. A postaccident examination found that the left main landing gear downlock link had separated. The airplane had experienced a previous landing gear collapse about three years before this accident. It had flown about six hours after extensive repairs and two annual inspections. The investigation could not determine whether the earlier landing gear collapse and this event were related.
