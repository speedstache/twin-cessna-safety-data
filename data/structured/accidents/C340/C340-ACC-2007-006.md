# Narrative Summary

The pilot flew a Part 91 business flight to a non-controlled airport located in a narrow river valley.
Weather in the broader region was VFR, but a localized dense fog layer covered the airport area and there were no published instrument approaches.
Witnesses reported the airplane maneuvered overhead and then flew low-level up the valley toward the airport before entering the fog.
About one mile from the airport, the pilot attempted to climb out of the valley and the airplane began impacting trees on rising terrain.
The airplane sustained damage from multiple tree impacts and then descended into terrain, followed by a post-crash fire.
Postaccident inspection of the airframe and engines found no evidence of a mechanical failure or malfunction that would have prevented normal operation.
