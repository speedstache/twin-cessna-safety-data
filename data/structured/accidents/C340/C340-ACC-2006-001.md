# Narrative Summary

A commercial pilot was arriving to land at Melbourne International Airport (KMLB) on a Part 91 business flight with two passengers. Air traffic control instructed the pilot to follow a slower airplane on base and requested that he reduce speed; the pilot acknowledged slowing and was then cleared to land. Witnesses observed the airplane flying slowly with the wings wobbling before it turned and descended into terrain on the extended final approach course. The aircraft was destroyed by impact forces and a postcrash fire, and all three occupants were fatally injured. Examination of the flight controls and engines found no evidence of preimpact mechanical malfunction. Toxicology identified diphenhydramine in the pilot’s blood at a level consistent with recent use of at least the maximum over-the-counter dose. The NTSB determined the accident was caused by the pilot’s failure to maintain adequate airspeed to avoid a stall on final approach, with pilot impairment due to a sedating antihistamine as a contributing factor.
