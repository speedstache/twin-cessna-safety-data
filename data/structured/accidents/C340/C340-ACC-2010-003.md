# Narrative Summary

On October 23, 2010, a Cessna 340A, N630PL, overran the runway at Camp Verde, Arizona, during a Part 91 personal flight. The pilot reported calm winds during the traffic pattern approach. The pilot turned base leg early, resulting in the airplane being higher than normal on final approach. The pilot reduced power and lowered the nose in an attempt to land with sufficient runway remaining. The airplane floated farther than expected, and the pilot believed he could still stop safely. The airplane overran the runway and came to rest on an embankment with collapsed landing gear and a bent wing. The pilot reported no mechanical malfunctions, and there were no injuries.
