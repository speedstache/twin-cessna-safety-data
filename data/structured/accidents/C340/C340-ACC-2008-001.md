# Narrative Summary

During a VFR arrival to runway 27 at Carl R Keller Field Airport (PCW), the airplane was observed flying very slowly with landing gear extended on downwind and base. The airplane stalled during the landing approach, entered a counterclockwise spin, and impacted terrain short of the runway in a residential area. An airport manager reported flying with the pilot eight days earlier and observed the stall warning horn sounding continuously during the approach and landing, with no corrective action taken. On the day of the accident, the pilot had approximately 1,160 hours total time, about 252 hours multiengine time, and about 12.6 hours in the accident airplane. The investigation found no preimpact mechanical anomalies and confirmed flight control continuity. Weather at the time was VMC with light winds and good visibility. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s failure to maintain sufficient airspeed to avoid a stall during the landing approach.
