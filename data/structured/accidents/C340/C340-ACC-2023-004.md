# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed St. Kitts for Providenciales with 160 gallons of fuel on board. The airplane had main wingtip tanks, in-wing auxiliary tanks, and an engine nacelle locker fuel tank. The pilot reported that his normal procedure was to use main tank fuel, then auxiliary tank fuel, then transfer and use the locker tank fuel. While approaching the destination, he attempted to transfer fuel from the locker tank and later noticed that fuel was not transferring. The right engine lost all power, followed by the left engine. The pilot ditched the airplane in the Atlantic Ocean about 17 miles from the destination airport. All four occupants donned life vests, exited the airplane, and were rescued about 4 hours later. The airplane was not recovered, and the fuel system could not be examined. The NTSB attributed the accident to an undetermined fuel system malfunction that resulted in fuel starvation to both engines.
