# Narrative Summary

During an IFR cross-country flight, the pilot was receiving vectors for an ILS approach to Montgomery-Gibbs Executive Airport with a planned circle-to-land. The airplane drifted right of the localizer while operating near cloud layers and the controller cancelled the approach clearance. The controller issued climb instructions and multiple low-altitude alerts. ADS-B data showed the airplane did not maintain the assigned climb and later entered a descending right turn. The airplane impacted a residential area and was destroyed by impact forces and postimpact fire. The pilot and one person on the ground were fatally injured, and two people on the ground were seriously injured. The airplane had received a modern avionics conversion about 11 months before the accident. The investigation found no airframe or engine anomaly that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB determined the probable cause was loss of control due to spatial disorientation.
