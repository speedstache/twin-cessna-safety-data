# Narrative Summary

The airplane was operated under Part 91 on a cross-country flight to McKinney National Airport. During the landing roll, the left main landing gear collapsed. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the aileron. Examination of the landing gear found the bell crank pivot bolt and gear down-lock broken. The Cessna 340 maintenance manual required landing gear system disassembly and inspection after 20 years or 10,000 landings and every 10 years or 5,000 landings thereafter. The manual also required recurring inspection of the main landing gear bell crank pivot bolt for wear. A review of the airplane maintenance records did not reveal an entry showing that the landing gear inspections had been accomplished. The airframe had accumulated 5,799.3 hours at the time of the last annual inspection. The NTSB determined that the bell crank pivot bolt failed and that lack of required landing gear inspections contributed to the accident.
