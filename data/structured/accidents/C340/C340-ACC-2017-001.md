# Narrative Summary

The pilot conducted a Part 91 positioning flight in deteriorating weather that included low visibility, freezing drizzle, and mist. Before departure, the pilot performed some before-takeoff checks while the airplane was in the hangar. During the preflight sequence, the pilot selected the wrong switch for windshield heat. While on an instrument approach at night, ice accumulation on the windshield eliminated forward visibility. The pilot executed a missed approach, contacted an instructor pilot to confirm the windshield heat switch location, and then activated the windshield heat. During the subsequent approach, the pilot increased airspeed because the airplane was carrying ice. On short final, the airplane descended below the glidepath and contacted terrain short of the runway. The airplane skipped onto the runway and came to rest upright off the runway surface. Examination revealed substantial damage to the left wing and no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions.
