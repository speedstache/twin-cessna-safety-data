# Narrative Summary

The pilot was cleared for a visual approach to his intended destination, which had a longer runway than the airport where the accident occurred.
He mistook Sonoma Valley Airport for his destination and landed on the 1,500-foot runway.
The pilot reported that wet runway conditions hindered braking effectiveness and the airplane hydroplaned during the landing roll.
The airplane overran the departure end of the runway and came to rest in a vineyard.
The pilot reported no preimpact mechanical malfunctions or failures with the airframe or engine.
The accident was attributed to misjudgment of speed and distance, with contributing factors including landing at the wrong airport and limited braking effectiveness on the wet runway.
