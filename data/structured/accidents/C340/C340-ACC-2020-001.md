# Narrative Summary

The pilot was conducting a Part 91 personal cross-country flight from Oklahoma to Texas. While climbing through 17,000 ft msl, the pilot completed a transition check. The pilot verified that cabin pressure altitude was 8,000 ft and that the pressure differential was within limits. As the airplane climbed through 18,000 ft msl, the right windscreen blew out. The pilot returned to the departure airport and landed without further incident. Six pieces of acrylic material comprising the outer circumference of the windscreen remained attached to the airframe. The blown-out section of windscreen was not recovered. Examination found fracture surfaces consistent with overstress separation. The available evidence did not identify the origin point or reason for the windscreen failure. The pilot's statement did not indicate a malfunction of the aircraft pressurization system.
