# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed Southwest Wyoming Regional Airport after calculating the density altitude to be 9,700 ft. Shortly after rotation, the pilot felt the airplane was slightly nose high and the stall warning horn activated. The airplane began to shudder, and the pilot attempted to abort the takeoff. The pilot reported difficulty lowering the nose to regain airspeed. About 10 ft above the ground, the airplane stalled and landed tail first before bouncing hard on the main landing gear. The right wing sustained substantial damage. The pilot reported no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions. The NTSB attributed the accident to improper pitch control during takeoff.
