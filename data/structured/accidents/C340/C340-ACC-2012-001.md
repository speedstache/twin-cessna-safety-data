# Narrative Summary

On January 27, 2012, a Cessna 340A impacted terrain short of runway 36 during final approach to Ocala International Airport, Florida. Radar data showed the airplane was about 1.16 miles from the runway and about 210 feet above ground level when it descended and impacted a pasture south of the airport. A passenger reported choppy conditions and hearing two distinct cockpit warning horns before the crash. Wreckage examination indicated the landing gear were in transit toward retraction at impact, consistent with an attempted go-around. The pilot made no distress calls to air traffic control before the accident. The pilot did not have a current flight review at the time of the accident. Postaccident examination and engine test runs found no evidence of a preexisting mechanical malfunction that would have precluded normal operation. The aircraft sustained substantial damage and a postcrash fire consumed the cockpit and cabin; the pilot was fatally injured and one passenger was seriously injured.
