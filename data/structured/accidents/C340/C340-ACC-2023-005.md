# Narrative Summary

The airplane touched down on both main landing gear during landing at Lakefront Airport in New Orleans. A few seconds after touchdown, the pilot felt that something failed on the left main landing gear. The left wing dropped and contacted the runway while the pilot attempted to maintain directional control. The airplane exited the runway surface. The left wing, left aileron, and horizontal stabilizer sustained substantial damage. Maintenance records showed the main landing gear had been overhauled about two months before the accident. The pilot had completed about 8 to 10 takeoffs and landings during the period after the overhaul. Postaccident examination found improper rigging and improper hardware installation in the main landing gear system. The NTSB determined that improper maintenance caused collapse and overstress failure of the left main landing gear during landing.
