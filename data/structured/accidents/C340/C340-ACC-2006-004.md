# Narrative Summary

A private pilot departed Rock Hill, South Carolina, on a Part 91 personal flight to Charleston Executive Airport (JZI) with three passengers. The flight was initially handled IFR and was cleared for an ILS approach, but the pilot canceled IFR services and continued visually. A UNICOM advisories provider reported the pilot made multiple runway selection statements and then flew a left base for runway 22 at low altitude. The airplane overshot the runway and entered a tight, low right turn; it subsequently stalled, spun, and impacted the Stono River. The pilot and three passengers were fatally injured, and the airplane sustained substantial damage. Examination of the airframe, flight controls, and engines found no evidence of a preimpact mechanical failure. Toxicology identified multiple drugs in the pilot’s specimens, including tramadol and diphenhydramine. The NTSB determined the accident was caused by the pilot’s failure to maintain airspeed during the turn from base to final, resulting in an inadvertent stall/spin, with pilot impairment due to drugs as a contributing factor.
