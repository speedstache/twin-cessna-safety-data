# Narrative Summary

The pilot was conducting a Part 91 executive/corporate flight on an instrument approach at night in instrument meteorological conditions. About 50 ft above the decision altitude, the pilot mistook building lights for runway lights. The airplane touched down in a soybean field about 3/4 mile short of the runway. The pilot then increased engine power to full, and the airplane climbed a couple hundred feet. After establishing visual contact with the runway lights, the pilot landed the airplane on the runway without further incident. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the left wing. The airport observation reported visibility of 1/2 statute mile with mist and an overcast ceiling at 200 ft agl shortly after the accident. The pilot reported fog, rain, and a ceiling near 300 ft agl. The pilot reported no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions.
