# Narrative Summary

On October 25, 2010, a Cessna 340A, N68718, experienced a loss of control in flight and impacted mountainous terrain near Morton, Washington, during a Part 91 business flight conducted under IFR. The flight departed Chehalis-Centralia Airport about 0725 and was destined for Lewiston, Idaho. About 14 minutes after departure, while climbing through 14,800 feet msl in instrument meteorological conditions, the pilot advised air traffic control that he was returning to the departure airport. Seconds later the pilot reported losing an engine and again stated he was returning. Radar data showed the airplane entering a right turn and then a rapid descending right turn before radar contact was lost. The airplane impacted a forested mountain slope in a near-vertical, slightly right-wing-low attitude. Postaccident examination did not identify mechanical malfunctions that would have precluded normal operation, and the reason for the partial loss of engine power was not determined. The pilot and two passengers sustained fatal injuries.
