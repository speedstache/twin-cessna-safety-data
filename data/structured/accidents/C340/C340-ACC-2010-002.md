# Narrative Summary

On February 27, 2010, a Cessna 340A, N1952E, departed the runway after landing at Grove City, Pennsylvania, during a Part 91 personal flight. The pilot overflew the airport and believed there was only a light coating of snow on the runway. After landing, the pilot observed about 1 to 1.5 inches of snow on the runway surface and the airplane began to slide to the right. The airplane departed the right side of the runway, struck a snow bank, and rotated about 180 degrees. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the horizontal stabilizer. The airport was unattended and no NOTAMs were issued regarding runway condition. The pilot and two passengers reported minor injuries.
