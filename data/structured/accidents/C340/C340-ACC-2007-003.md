# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed to troubleshoot a landing gear anomaly and cycled the landing gear, then returned toward the departure airport.
While about 2 miles from the airport and approximately 1,800 ft agl, the right engine began to lose power.
The pilot attempted a restart, cycled the fuel pump, and selected the right auxiliary fuel tank; the right engine regained some power.
After losing altitude during troubleshooting, the pilot raised the landing gear to reduce drag and entered right traffic for the runway.
The left engine then lost power, and the pilot feathered the left propeller and switched to another fuel tank, but the engine did not regain power.
With insufficient altitude to maintain airspeed and reach the runway, the pilot forced-landed on a residential street and the airplane struck houses and the street.
An FAA inspector noted little evidence of fuel on board the wreckage, and the accident was attributed to fuel starvation from inadequate fuel management.
