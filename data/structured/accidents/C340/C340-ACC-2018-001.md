# Narrative Summary

The pilot began a Part 91 personal flight from Orlando Executive Airport using the full length of a dry, paved 6,000-ft runway. Engine start, run-up, and taxi were reported as normal. Shortly after the takeoff roll began, the pilot felt a sudden lag in forward motion and observed a maximum airspeed indication of 43 knots with a fluttering needle. The pilot verified that the mixture, propeller, and throttle controls were fully forward. The pilot decided to discontinue the takeoff about halfway down the runway. The airplane did not appear likely to stop before the runway end, so the pilot steered it to the right as it overran the runway. The airplane impacted and stopped in a culvert about 500 ft beyond the runway and sustained substantial damage to the fuselage nose. Postaccident examination did not reveal a preimpact mechanical malfunction that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB determined that the pilot delayed discontinuing the takeoff when confronted with abnormal acceleration and airspeed indication.
