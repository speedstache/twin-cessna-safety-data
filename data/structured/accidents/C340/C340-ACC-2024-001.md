# Narrative Summary

During a night cross-country flight to Muskogee, Oklahoma, the pilot reported that the Cessna 340A had been topped off before departure. The pilot performed the preflight inspection at night using a cellular phone and later reported that he did not recall verifying the right main fuel tank level before departure. The pilot calculated that the flight would require 150 gallons and reported higher than normal fuel consumption during the first half of the flight. While enroute, he transferred fuel from auxiliary and locker tanks and calculated that adequate fuel, including reserve, remained to reach the destination. Near Tulsa, about 50 miles from the destination, the pilot observed that the indicated fuel levels were lower than expected. Shortly after clearance for a visual approach, the right engine lost total power, followed by the left engine. The pilot attempted engine restarts without success and performed a forced landing. During the landing, the right wing struck a static display aircraft on airport property, and the airplane sustained substantial damage to the fuselage and right wing. Postaccident examination found no usable fuel aboard and no signs of fuel leakage. The NTSB determined that improper fuel planning resulted in fuel exhaustion and total loss of power to both engines.
