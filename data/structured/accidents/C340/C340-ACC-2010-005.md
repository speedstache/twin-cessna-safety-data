# Narrative Summary

On December 21, 2010, a Cessna 340A, N233DS, experienced an aerodynamic stall during an instrument approach at Madison, Wisconsin, during a Part 91 positioning flight. The pilot attempted two landings at the original destination airport but elected to return to the departure airport due to weather. While on the instrument approach, the windshield became covered with ice and the de-ice system did not clear the windshield. The pilot attempted to land by looking out the left window. About 15 feet above the runway, the pilot observed an airspeed of about 85 knots and the airplane stalled. The pilot added engine power and the airplane made a hard landing. Examination found the forward fuselage buckled and the de-ice fluid tank empty, with no mechanical anomalies noted. The pilot stated he did not check the de-ice fluid level during the preflight inspection, and there were no injuries.
