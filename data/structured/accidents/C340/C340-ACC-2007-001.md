# Narrative Summary

The pilot conducted a VOR-A instrument approach at night to Council Bluffs Municipal Airport under instrument meteorological conditions.
Radar data showed the airplane crossed the VOR at 2,800 ft msl and then deviated from the published inbound course while descending.
The airplane descended below the published minimum descent altitude for the approach while maneuvering.
Weather information for the area indicated potential moderate turbulence and low-level wind shear, and icing potential consistent with likely severe icing during descent and approach.
The pilot had obtained a preflight weather briefing that included AIRMETs and pilot reports for icing and turbulence along the route.
The airplane impacted terrain and trees about 3 nautical miles south-southeast of the destination airport and was destroyed.
Postaccident inspection of the airframe and engines did not identify a preimpact mechanical failure or malfunction.
