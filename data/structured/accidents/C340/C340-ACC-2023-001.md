# Narrative Summary

The pilot was taxiing for departure at Boise Air Terminal/Gowen Field during night visual meteorological conditions. The airplane drifted left of the taxiway centerline and the left wingtip struck a fence. The impact breached the left wingtip fuel tank and a postimpact fire occurred. The pilot reported that the right brake failed while he attempted to stop. Examination of the brake system, nosewheel steering system, wheels, and tires found no failure or malfunction that would have precluded normal operation. Airport surveillance video showed the airplane moving at a relatively constant speed and gradually drifting left before impact. The pilot was not injured, and the airplane sustained substantial damage. The NTSB attributed the accident to failure to maintain directional control while taxiing.
