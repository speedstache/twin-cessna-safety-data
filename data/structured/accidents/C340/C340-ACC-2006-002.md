# Narrative Summary

A private pilot was conducting a Part 91 personal flight to San Antonio, Texas. On final approach the pilot extended the landing gear and reported observing three green down-and-locked indications. After touchdown, the right main landing gear collapsed and the airplane sustained substantial damage; the two occupants were not injured. The pilot stated the landing gear system had been recently maintained during an annual inspection completed in May 2006. An FAA inspector found that the pivot bolt on the right main landing gear down-lock bellcrank fulcrum had failed. The reason for the bolt failure could not be determined. The NTSB determined the accident resulted from a main landing gear collapse due to the pivot bolt failure for undetermined reasons.
