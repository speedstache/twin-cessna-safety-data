# Narrative Summary

On January 18, 2010, a Cessna 340, N2217B, collided with mountainous terrain near Lytle Creek, California, during a Part 91 personal cross-country flight. The flight departed Henderson, Nevada, about 1303 with a planned destination of Compton, California, and no flight plan was filed. The pilot did not hold an instrument rating. Recorded radar data showed the airplane maneuvering with multiple turns and altitude variations while operating near low cloud bases in mountainous terrain. A witness reported low altitude flight beneath the cloud layer with no apparent mechanical distress. The airplane impacted terrain and both occupants sustained fatal injuries. Postaccident examination of the airframe and engines revealed no mechanical failures or malfunctions that would have precluded normal operation.
