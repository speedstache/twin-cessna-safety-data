# Narrative Summary

The pilot was returning to the airport after collecting air samples at various altitudes over oil fields. While being vectored for an instrument approach, the airplane overshot the runway’s extended centerline and the pilot reported a fire on board. The airplane then lost altitude rapidly and radar contact was lost. The airplane struck the ground at high velocity and low impact angle and was destroyed; the pilot was fatally injured. Examination of windshield fragments found soot streaking on an exterior surface that did not extend into fracture surfaces, indicating smoke exposure before breakup at impact. Both nose baggage compartment doors were found about 2 miles south of the main wreckage, suggesting they separated before the distress call. Post-accident examination found no evidence the installed air sampling system caused the fire. A combustion heater mounted in the right front section of the nose baggage compartment showed a loose fuel fitting. The NTSB determined that a loose fuel fitting on the combustion heater leaked a lean fuel-air mixture into the nose baggage compartment and the mixture was most likely ignited by the heater, which blew off the doors and started an in-flight fire.
