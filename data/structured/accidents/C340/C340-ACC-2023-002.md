# Narrative Summary

The pilot had moved the airplane from his hangar and started both engines for a planned flight from Hayward. While setting up the avionics, the pilot suddenly lost consciousness. The airplane began moving across the taxiway while the passenger attempted to wake him. The airplane continued across a taxiway intersection and into a runway safety area before colliding with a swale. The nose landing gear collapsed and both wings sustained substantial damage. The pilot later reported no preaccident mechanical malfunction or failure. Medical evaluation attributed the loss of consciousness to an abnormal heartbeat associated with previously undiagnosed heart disease. The NTSB determined that the pilot’s sudden incapacitation caused the loss of control and impact with terrain.
