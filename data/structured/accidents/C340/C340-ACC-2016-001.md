# Narrative Summary

The pilot configured the airplane to land and observed an indication that the right main landing gear was not locked in the extended position. The pilot left the traffic pattern and attempted to troubleshoot by cycling the landing gear without success. The pilot returned to the airport and used the hand crank to manually deploy the gear but continued to receive a “gear unlocked” indication. The airplane touched down normally, but the right main landing gear collapsed as the pilot attempted a right turn to exit the runway, resulting in substantial damage to the right aileron. Postaccident examination found the right main landing gear aft drive tube failed in tensile overload. The airplane manufacturer stated that low overcenter tension, typically due to improper rigging, can lead to main landing gear collapse under load and aft drive tube fracture in overload. Maintenance records indicated that downlock tension adjustments were made in 2011, 2012, and 2013, and maintenance personnel did not re-rig the landing gear as required by the service manual after those adjustments. The NTSB determined that maintenance personnel’s repeated failure to re-rig the main landing gear in accordance with the manufacturer’s requirements resulted in the collapse of the right main landing gear.
