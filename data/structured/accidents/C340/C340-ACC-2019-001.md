# Narrative Summary

The pilot was departing a private dirt airstrip on a Part 91 personal flight. During the short-field takeoff, the airplane encountered soft patches of dirt. The pilot rotated to initiate a climb, but the airplane could not climb higher than ground effect and the stall horn sounded. The pilot lowered the nose to gain airspeed. The landing gear then impacted a gravel berm and was sheared off. The airplane skidded about 150 ft and came to rest in a dirt field past the end of the runway. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the left wing and right horizontal stabilizer. The pilot reported that there were no preaccident mechanical failures or malfunctions. Before takeoff, the pilot determined that about 5,000 ft of runway was usable, but the runway was actually about 3,500 ft long. The pilot reported that airport elevation was about 5,000 ft msl and density altitude was about 8,000 ft.
