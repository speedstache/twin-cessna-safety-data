# Narrative Summary

The pilot was conducting a Part 91 business flight on an IFR flight plan at night. During the instrument approach to Port Huron, the right engine lost total power about 1.3 nautical miles from the final approach fix. The pilot reported the engine loss to air traffic control and continued the approach. He later reported that he could not activate the airport runway lights and indicated that he was over the airport and would reshoot the approach. The last radar return showed the airplane about 450 ft agl at 72 knots groundspeed. The airplane impacted the ground in a steep, vertical nose-down attitude about 1/2 nautical mile from the departure end of the runway. The landing gear and flaps were extended, and the right propeller was not feathered. Engine data indicated that the right engine was not secured and that the left engine continued to produce power until impact. Fuel remained on board the airplane, but the evidence supported fuel starvation of the right engine. The NTSB cited improper fuel management, inadequate flight planning, failure to secure the right engine, and failure to configure the airplane for the go-around.
