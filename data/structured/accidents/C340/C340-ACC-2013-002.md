# Narrative Summary

On October 4, 2013, a Cessna 340A collided with a radio tower while maneuvering at low altitude near Paulden, Arizona. Witnesses reported the airplane made a high-speed low pass over a gun club and then maneuvered for a second low pass. During the second pass, the airplane struck a radio tower about 50 feet tall, and the right wing sheared off a portion of the tower. After the impact, the airplane rolled right to an almost inverted attitude and then impacted trees and terrain about 700 feet southwest of the initial impact point. A witness reported the airplane remained straight-and-level until the tower collision and stated the pilot had previously "buzzed" the club several years earlier. The private pilot and three passengers were fatally injured, and the airplane sustained substantial damage and a postcrash fire. Postaccident examination of the engines and airframe found no evidence of a mechanical malfunction that would have precluded normal operation. The flight was operated under Part 91 and no flight plan was filed.
