# Narrative Summary

The airplane departed Bermuda Dunes Airport (UDD) on a VFR cross-country flight to Chino Airport (CNO) and was receiving VFR flight following. While transiting a mountainous pass near Cabazon, California, the airplane encountered instrument meteorological conditions and collided with upsloping mountainous terrain in controlled flight. Radar data showed the airplane climbing to about 6,900 ft msl, then descending in a right turn to about 5,800 ft msl before radar contact was lost near the accident site. A pilot transiting the same pass reported overcast conditions with a ceiling around 4,000 ft msl and tops at 7,000 ft msl or higher. Postaccident examination of the airframe and engines found no anomalies that would have precluded normal operation. The airplane was destroyed and the pilot and three passengers were fatally injured. The NTSB determined the probable cause was the pilot’s continued VFR flight into IMC and failure to maintain terrain clearance en route, with clouds and mountainous terrain contributing.
