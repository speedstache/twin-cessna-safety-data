# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed on a personal cross-country flight and did not visually inspect the fuel level in the tanks before departure.
About 15 minutes into the flight, he observed erratic fuel gauge indications.
The left engine lost power and then regained power without pilot action; shortly thereafter the right engine lost power and restart attempts were unsuccessful.
The left engine then lost power again and restart attempts were unsuccessful.
The pilot conducted a forced landing in the desert near Interstate 40 and the airplane struck rocks during the landing roll.
Postaccident examination found the right main tank was intact and empty, the left main tank was punctured with only a trace of fuel, and both auxiliary tanks contained fuel.
Both fuel selectors were positioned to the right main tank; the POH required selecting the auxiliary tanks to use the fuel contained in them.
