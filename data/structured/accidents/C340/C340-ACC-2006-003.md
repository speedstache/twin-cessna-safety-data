# Narrative Summary

An airline transport-rated pilot receiving instruction and a commercial flight instructor departed on a local Part 91 instructional flight from Glacier Park International Airport (GPI), Kalispell, Montana. During the second approach configuration, the crew heard a loud bang while extending the landing gear; the main gear indicated down, but the nose gear did not extend and the unsafe light remained on. The crew performed the emergency landing gear checklist and completed two tower flybys, and tower personnel confirmed the nose gear remained retracted. The crew shut down both engines on short final and landed on the main gear, allowing the airplane to settle onto its nose as airspeed decreased. The airplane came to rest about 2,000 feet down the runway and sustained substantial damage; both pilots were uninjured. Post-accident examination found the nose gear adjusting bellcrank fractured in overstress and evidence consistent with binding in a linkage forward of the bellcrank, though the cause of the binding could not be determined. The NTSB determined the probable cause was an overstress fracture of the adjusting bellcrank due to linkage binding.
