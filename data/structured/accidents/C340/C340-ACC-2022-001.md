# Narrative Summary

The Cessna 340A pilot was inbound to Watsonville Municipal Airport on a straight-in approach to runway 20. The pilot made CTAF position reports, and a Cessna 152 in the traffic pattern also made position reports. The Cessna 340A maintained about 180 knots groundspeed until the collision. The Cessna 152 pilot announced a go-around after recognizing that the Cessna 340A was closing rapidly. The airplanes collided on final approach about 150 ft above ground level. The Cessna 340A landing gear and flaps were found retracted. The Cessna 340A pilot and passenger were fatally injured, and the airplane was destroyed. The investigation found no mechanical malfunction that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB attributed the accident to the Cessna 340A pilot’s failure to see and avoid the Cessna 152.
