# Narrative Summary

The pilot was operating under Part 91 on an IFR flight plan to Angel Fire Airport (AXX) at night and reported being cleared for the GPS 17 approach. The pilot stated he broke out of the clouds at about 1,800 feet and was high and too close to land, then executed a circling maneuver and entered a close left traffic pattern. Witnesses observed the airplane fly along the runway at about 200–300 feet above ground, add power to go around, then enter downwind, descend gradually, turn left base, and disappear behind a hill. The airplane impacted terrain about one mile northeast of the airport and a post-impact fire destroyed the airplane; the pilot sustained minor injuries. METARs in the area reported 10 miles visibility with cloud layers present, and rain began shortly after the accident. Examination of the airplane systems found no anomalies and no evidence of an in-flight fire. The NTSB determined the accident was controlled flight into terrain for unknown reasons.
