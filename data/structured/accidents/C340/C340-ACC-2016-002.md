# Narrative Summary

Two airplanes departed from converging runways at a nontowered airport at about the same time: a Cessna 340A on an IFR departure from runway 4 and a Cessna 172M on a VFR repositioning departure from runway 36. Airport video showed the Cessna 172M lifting off and approaching the runway intersection as the Cessna 340A was just above runway 4 and approaching the same intersection with the landing gear extended. The Cessna 340A then began a climbing left turn with an increasing bank angle while the Cessna 172M continued straight ahead. The Cessna 340A rolled inverted and impacted the ground in a nose-low, left-wing-low attitude and was destroyed; both occupants were fatally injured. The Cessna 340A was likely being flown near the published takeoff and climb speed of 93 knots indicated airspeed. The published stall speed for the airplane at a 40-degree bank was 93 knots, and the airplane likely exceeded the critical angle of attack during the maneuver and entered an aerodynamic stall. Wreckage examination did not reveal any preimpact mechanical malfunctions that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB determined that the pilot intentionally maneuvered at low altitude during takeoff in response to a near-miss with the other airplane, resulting in an aerodynamic stall.
