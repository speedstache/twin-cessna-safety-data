# Narrative Summary

The pilot overflew the destination airport and observed blowing snow on both runways. The pilot elected to land on runway 22 due to wind from about 220 degrees. During the landing rollout, the airplane collided with plowed snow at the intersection with runway 07/25. The impact collapsed the nose landing gear and the airplane sustained substantial fuselage damage. The automated weather observation about 5 minutes before the accident reported 3 miles visibility in light snow with wind from 260 degrees at 11 knots and a broken ceiling at 1,200 feet AGL. Airport directory notes indicated that runway 07/25 was plowed during winter months and to contact the airport manager for conditions. The automated weather broadcast normally included that directory message, but an electrical power interruption erased the message prior to the accident. The NTSB determined that airport personnel did not remove the snow banks from the intersecting runway and/or close the intersection runway, and that reduced visibility inhibited the pilot’s ability to see the plowed snow hazard.
