# Narrative Summary

The pilot departed St. Petersburg-Clearwater International Airport, flew one circuit in the traffic pattern, and landed on runway 18. The touchdown occurred about 500 to 1,000 ft beyond the first runway intersection. During the landing rollout, the pilot reported that the brakes were not working and that he could not stop or slow the airplane on the remaining runway. The airplane veered off the left side of the runway and continued about 1,800 ft before striking the airport perimeter fence. The fuselage sustained substantial damage. The pilot was not injured. Postaccident examination of the brakes found no preimpact mechanical malfunction or failure that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB attributed the accident to the pilot’s inability to stop the airplane on the available runway.
