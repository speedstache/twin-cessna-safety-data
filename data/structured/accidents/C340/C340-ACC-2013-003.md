# Narrative Summary

On October 10, 2013, a Cessna 340A collided with terrain near Hampton Roads Executive Airport, Virginia, during a missed approach in instrument meteorological conditions. Radar data showed the airplane deviated from assigned course and altitude during vectors to an intersection for a GPS approach and crossed the final approach fix below the published minimum crossing altitude. The pilot descended to the minimum descent altitude and initiated the missed approach. Radar data indicated the airplane then flew away from the published missed approach course, with multiple altitude excursions and turns before entering a tightening right turn and descending into terrain. During the deviations, the pilot reported to air traffic control that adverse weather was causing the airplane to lose large amounts of altitude, though weather radar did not show heavy precipitation at the airplane’s location. Recorded weather near the destination included low ceilings and reduced visibility with rain. The investigation could not corroborate the pilot’s reported flight time or document recent actual instrument experience, and it could not determine the pilot’s proficiency with the GPS equipment installed about six months before the accident. Wreckage examination found no evidence of any preimpact mechanical malfunctions or failures.
