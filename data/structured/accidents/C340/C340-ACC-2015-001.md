# Narrative Summary

The pilot was departing runway 16 on an IFR flight plan. After traveling down about two-thirds of the 4,002-foot runway, the pilot aborted the takeoff after he felt the airplane was not able to climb and continue to accelerate. During the aborted takeoff, the airplane departed the runway surface and entered a grass overrun area that was wet with morning dew. The pilot attempted to stop the airplane, but the airplane skidded and impacted a fence. The airplane sustained substantial damage to the right main wing spar and fuselage. Postaccident examination revealed no mechanical malfunctions or failures that would have precluded normal operations. The NTSB determined that the reason the airplane was unable to take off could not be determined, and that the aborted takeoff resulted in the runway overrun and impact with a fence.
