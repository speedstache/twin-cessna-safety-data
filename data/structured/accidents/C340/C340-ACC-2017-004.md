# Narrative Summary

The instrument-rated private pilot and four passengers boarded the airplane for a Part 91 personal flight from Bartow, Florida. Dense fog limited visibility, and the pilot requested that the airplane be towed from the hangar to the ramp to avoid taxiing near other hangars. Witnesses heard the engine run-up and takeoff, but the airplane was not visible due to fog. Weather at the time included visibility less than 1/4 mile in fog and an overcast ceiling at 300 ft agl. The airplane was calculated to be about 105 lb over maximum takeoff weight and outside the center-of-gravity moment envelope. After departure, the airplane impacted terrain inverted in a nearly vertical nose-low attitude near the departure end of the runway. Engine examination showed signatures consistent with takeoff power at impact. No airframe, engine, or cockpit instrumentation anomaly was found that would have precluded normal operation. The NTSB determined that the pilot lost control due to spatial disorientation during takeoff in instrument meteorological conditions.
